Sorensen also said that the president often expressed impatience with Rusk and felt him under-prepared for emergency meetings and crises.[33] As Rusk recounted in his autobiography, he repeatedly offered his resignation, but it was never accepted.[34]  Rumors of Rusk's dismissal leading up to the 1964 election abounded prior to President Kennedy's trip to Dallas in 1963. Shortly after Kennedy was assassinated, Rusk offered his resignation to the new president, Lyndon B. Johnson.[35]  However, Johnson liked Rusk and refused his resignation. He remained secretary throughout Johnson's administration.[35]
