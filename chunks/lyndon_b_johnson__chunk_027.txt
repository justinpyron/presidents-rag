Baker resigned in October, and the investigation did not expand to Johnson. The negative publicity, however, fed rumors in Washington circles that Kennedy was planning on dropping Johnson from the Democratic ticket in the 1964 presidential election. However, on October 31, 1963, a reporter asked if he intended and expected to retain Johnson on the ticket. Kennedy replied, "Yes to both those questions."[12] There is little doubt that Robert Kennedy and Johnson hated each other,[13] yet John and Robert Kennedy agreed that dropping Johnson from the ticket could produce heavy losses in the South.[14][15]
