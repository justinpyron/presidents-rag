Shannon retained his position as Counselor until February 12, 2016, when he was appointed as Under Secretary of State for Political Affairs for the last year of Barack Obama's presidency.[1][4][5][6]
Acting Secretary of State
After Donald Trump was inaugurated 45th President of the United States on January 20, 2017, Shannon became Acting United States Secretary of State. He remained in office for only 12 days, when on February 1, 2017, the Senate confirmed Rex Tillerson, President Trump's nominee, as Secretary of State.[7][8]
While Shannon was acting Secretary of State, President Trump dismissed many prominent and senior State Department officials, resulting in the firing or forced resignations of over a dozen career diplomats,[9] leaving a majority of senior career positions at the Department vacant.[10]  Most notably, Thomas Countryman, acting Undersecretary for Arms Control and International Security, was on his way to Rome for an international meeting on nuclear weapons when he discovered that he had been summarily removed from his position. Without leaving the airport, he turned around and got on the first flight back to Washington.[11]
Under Secretary for Political Affairs
On February 2, 2016, the Senate confirmed Shannon as Under Secretary of State for Political Affairs.[12]
