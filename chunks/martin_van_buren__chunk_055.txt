His presidency is considered to be average, at best, by historians. He was blamed for the Panic of 1837 and defeated for reelection.[2] His tenure was dominated by the economic conditions caused by the panic, and historians have split on the adequacy of the Independent Treasury as a response.[4] Some historians disagree with these negative assessments.
