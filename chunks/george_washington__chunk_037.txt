Demobilization and resignation
Main article: George Washington's resignation as commander-in-chief
When peace negotiations began in April 1782, both the British and French began gradually evacuating their forces.[107] With the American treasury empty, unpaid and mutinous soldiers forced the adjournment of Congress. In March 1783, Washington successfully calmed the Newburgh Conspiracy, a planned munity by American officers; Congress promised each a five-year bonus.[108] Washington submitted an account of $450,000 in expenses which he had advanced to the army, equivalent to $9.53Â million in 2023. The account was settled, though it was allegedly vague about large sums and included expenses his wife had incurred through visits to his headquarters.[109]
The following month, a Congressional committee led by Alexander Hamilton began adapting the army for peacetime. In August 1783, Washington gave the Army's perspective to the committee in his Sentiments on a Peace Establishment, which advised Congress to keep a standing army, create a "national militia" of separate state units, and establish a navy and a national military academy.[110]
