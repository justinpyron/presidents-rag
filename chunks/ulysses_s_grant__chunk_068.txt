Grant's veto placed him in the Republican conservative faction and began the party's commitment to a gold-backed dollar.[165] Grant later pressured Congress for a bill to strengthen the dollar by gradually reducing the greenbacks in circulation. When the Democrats gained a majority in the House after the 1874 elections, the lame-duck Republican Congress did so before the Democrats took office.[166] On January 14, 1875, Grant signed the Specie Payment Resumption Act, which required reduction of greenbacks allowed to circulate and declared that they would be redeemed for gold beginning on January 1, 1879.[167]
Reforms and scandals
Further information: Reforms of the Ulysses S. Grant administration and Scandals of the Ulysses S. Grant administration
The post-Civil War economy brought on massive industrial wealth and government expansion. Speculation, lifestyle extravagance, and corruption in federal offices were rampant.[168] All of Grant's executive departments were investigated by Congress.[169] Grant by nature was honest, trusting, gullible, and loyal to his friends. His responses to malfeasance were mixed: at times appointing cabinet reformers, others defending culprits.[170]
