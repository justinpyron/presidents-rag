In late October 1929, the stock market crashed, and the worldwide economy began to spiral downward into the Great Depression.[12] The causes of the Great Depression remain a matter of debate,[13] but Hoover viewed a lack of confidence in the financial system as the fundamental economic problem facing the nation.[14] He sought to avoid direct federal intervention, believing that the best way to bolster the economy was through the strengthening of businesses such as banks and railroads.
