World War I

Shortly after the American entry into World War I in April 1917, Marshall had roles as a planner of both training and operations.[1] In the summer, he was assigned as assistant chief of staff for operations on the staff of the newly created 1st Division, commanded by Major General William L. Sibert, a fifty-six-year-old engineer officer.[2] After overseeing the division's mobilization and organization in Texas, he departed for France with the division staff in mid-1917.[1] On the long ocean voyage, his roommate was the division's assistant chief of staff for training, Major Lesley J.
