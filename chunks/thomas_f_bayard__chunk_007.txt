Specie resumption
From the start of his congressional career, Bayard was an advocate of hard money, i.e., a dollar backed by gold.[11] During the Civil War, Congress had authorized a new form of currency, redeemable not in specie (gold or silver coin) but in 6% government bonds.[12] These United States Notes, popularly known as "greenbacks," had helped to finance the war when the government's gold supply did not keep pace with the expanding costs of maintaining the armies.
