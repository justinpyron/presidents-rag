On August 12, 1944, Kennedy's older brother, Joe Jr., a navy pilot, was killed on an air mission. His body was never recovered.[28][29] The news reached the family's home in Hyannis Port, Massachusetts, a day later. Kennedy felt that Joe Jr.'s reckless flight was partly an effort to outdo him.[30][31] To console himself, Kennedy set out to assemble a privately published book of remembrances of his brother, As We Remember Joe.[32]
Journalism (1945)
In April 1945, Kennedy's father, who was a friend of William Randolph Hearst, arranged a position for his son as a special correspondent for Hearst Newspapers; the assignment kept Kennedy's name in the public eye and "expose[d] him to journalism as a possible career."[1] That May he went to Berlin as a correspondent,[2] covering the Potsdam Conference and other events.[3]
