Entertainment career
Further information: Ronald Reagan filmography
Radio and film
After obtaining a Bachelor of Arts degree in economics and sociology from Eureka College in 1932,[1][2] Reagan took a job in Davenport, Iowa, as a sports broadcaster for four football games in the Big Ten Conference.[3] He then worked for WHO radio in Des Moines as a broadcaster for the Chicago Cubs. His specialty was creating play-by-play accounts of games using only basic descriptions that the station received by wire as the games were in progress.[4] Simultaneously, he often expressed his opposition to racism.[5] In 1936, while traveling with the Cubs to their spring training in California, Reagan took a screen test that led to a seven-year contract with Warner Bros.[6]
Reagan arrived at Hollywood in 1937, debuting in Love Is on the Air (1937).[7] Using a simple and direct approach to acting and following his directors' instructions,[8] Reagan made thirty films, mostly B films, before beginning military service in April 1942.[9] He broke out of these types of films by portraying George Gipp in Knute Rockne, All American (1940), which would be rejuvenated when reporters called Reagan "the Gipper" while he campaigned for president of the United States.[10] Afterward, Reagan starred in Kings Row (1942) as a leg amputee, asking, "Where's the rest of me?"[11] His performance was considered his bes
