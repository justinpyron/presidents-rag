He was inspired both by the Commandant, Captain Charles Ferguson Smith, and by General Winfield Scott, who visited the academy to review the cadets. Grant later wrote of the military life, "there is much to dislike, but more to like."[12]
Grant graduated on June 30, 1843, ranked 21st out of 39 in his class and was promoted the next day to brevet second lieutenant.[13] He planned to resign his commission after his four-year term. He would later write that among the happiest days of his life were the day he left the presidency and the day he left the academy.[14] Despite his excellent horsemanship, he was not assigned to the cavalry, but to the 4th Infantry Regiment.[b] Grant's first assignment was the Jefferson Barracks near St. Louis, Missouri.[16] Commanded by Colonel Stephen W.
