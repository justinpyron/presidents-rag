Main article: Social policy of Donald Trump
Trump barred organizations that provide abortions or abortion referrals from receiving federal funds.[49] He said he supported "traditional marriage", but considered the nationwide legality of same-sex marriage "settled".[50] His administration rolled back key components of the Obama administration's workplace protections against discrimination of LGBTQ people.[51] Trump's attempted rollback of anti-discrimination protections for transgender patients in August 2020 was halted by a federal judge after a Supreme Court ruling extended employees' civil rights protections to gender identity and sexual orientation.[52]
Trump has said he is opposed to gun control, although his views have shifted over time.[53] After several mass shootings during his term, he said he would propose legislation related to guns, but he abandoned that effort in November 2019.[54] His administration took an anti-marijuana position, revoking Obama-era policies that provided protections for states that legalized marijuana.[55]
Trump is a long-time advocate of capital punishment.[56][57] Under his administration, the federal government executed 13 prisoners, more than in the previous 56 years combined, ending a 17-year moratorium.[58] In 2016, Trump said he supported the use of interrogation torture methods such as waterboarding.[59][60]
