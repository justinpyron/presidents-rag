Biographer Richard Reeves said that Kennedy focused primarily on the political repercussions of the plan rather than military considerations. When it proved unsuccessful, he was convinced that the plan was a setup to make him look bad.[48] He took responsibility for the failure, saying, "We got a big kick in the leg and we deserved it. But maybe we'll learn something from it."[49] Kennedy's approval ratings climbed afterwards, helped in part by the vocal support given to him by Nixon and Eisenhower.[50] He appointed Robert Kennedy to help lead a committee to examine the causes of the failure.[51] The Kennedy administration banned all Cuban imports and convinced the Organization of American States (OAS) to expel Cuba.[52]
