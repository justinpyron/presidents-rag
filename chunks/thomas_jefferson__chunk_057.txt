Final days, death, and burial
See also: Death of John Adams
Jefferson's approximately $100,000 of debt weighed heavily on his mind in his final months,[citation needed] as it became increasingly clear that he would have little to leave to his heirs. In February 1826, he successfully applied to the General Assembly to hold a public lottery as a fundraiser.[29] His health began to deteriorate in July 1825, due to a combination of rheumatism from arm and wrist injuries, and intestinal and urinary disorders.[5] By June 1826, he was confined to bed.[29] On July 3, overcome by fever, Jefferson declined an invitation to attend an anniversary celebration of the Declaration in Washington.[30]
During his last hours, he was accompanied by family members and friends.
