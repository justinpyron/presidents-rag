Presidential election of 1908
See also: 1908 United States presidential election
Gaining the nomination

Roosevelt had served almost three and a half years of McKinley's term. On the night of his own election in 1904, Roosevelt publicly declared that he would not run for reelection in 1908, a pledge he quickly regretted. But he felt bound by his word. Roosevelt believed Taft was his logical successor, although the War Secretary had initially been reluctant to run.[1] Roosevelt used his control of the party machinery to aid his heir apparent.[1] On pain of the loss of their jobs, political appointees were required to support Taft or remain silent.[2]
