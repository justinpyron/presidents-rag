The period of American history most dominated by Reagan and his policies (particularly on taxes, welfare, defense, the federal judiciary, and the Cold War) is known as the Reagan era, which suggests that the "Reagan Revolution" had a lasting impact on the United States in domestic and foreign policy. The George H. W. Bush and Bill Clinton administrations  are often treated as an extension of the era, as is the George W. Bush administration.[29] Since 1988, Republican presidential candidates have invoked Reagan's policies and beliefs.[30]
