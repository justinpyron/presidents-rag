In 1778, Jefferson was given the task of revising the state's laws. He drafted 126 bills in three years, including laws to streamline the judicial system. He proposed statutes that provided for general education, which he considered the basis of "republican government".[15] Jefferson also was concerned that Virginia's powerful landed gentry were becoming a hereditary aristocracy and took the lead in abolishing what he called "feudal and unnatural distinctions."[19] He targeted laws such as entail and primogeniture by which a deceased landowner's oldest son was vested with all land ownership and power.[19][c]
Jefferson was elected governor for one-year terms in 1779 and 1780.[21] He transferred the state capital from Williamsburg to Richmond, and introduced additional measures for public education, religious freedom, and inheritance.[22]
