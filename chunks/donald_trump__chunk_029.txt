Conflicts of interest
See also: First presidency of Donald Trump § Ethics
Before being inaugurated, Trump moved his businesses into a revocable trust,[4][5] rather than a blind trust or equivalent arrangement "to cleanly sever himself from his business interests".[6] Trump continued to profit from his businesses and to know how his administration's policies affected his businesses.[5][7] Although he said he would eschew "new foreign deals", the Trump Organization pursued expansions of its operations in Dubai, Scotland, and the Dominican Republic.[5][7]
Trump was sued for violating the Domestic and Foreign Emoluments Clauses of the U.S. Constitution, marking the first time that the clauses had been substantively litigated.[8] One case was dismissed in lower court.[9] Two were dismissed by the U.S. Supreme Court as moot after the end of Trump's term.[10]
