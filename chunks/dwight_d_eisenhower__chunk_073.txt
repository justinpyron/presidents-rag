During a visit to England, he complained of dizziness and had to have his blood pressure checked on August 29, 1959; however, before dinner at prime ministerial manor house Chequers on the next day his physician, General Howard Snyder, recalled that Eisenhower "drank several gin-and-tonics, and one or two gins on the rocksÂ ... three or four wines with the dinner".[113]
Eisenhower's health during the last three years of his second term in office was relatively good. After leaving the White House, he suffered several additional and ultimately crippling heart attacks.[114] A severe heart attack in August 1965 largely ended his participation in public affairs.[115] On December 12, 1966, his gallbladder was removed, containing 16 gallstones.[114] After Eisenhower's death in 1969, an autopsy revealed an undiagnosed adrenal pheochromocytoma,[116] a benign adrenalin-secreting tumor that may have made him more vulnerable to heart disease. Eisenhower had seven heart attacks from 1955 until his death.[114]
End of presidency
The 22nd Amendment to the US Constitution, which set a two-term limit on the presidency, was ratified in 1951. Eisenhower was the first president constitutionally prevented from serving a third term.
