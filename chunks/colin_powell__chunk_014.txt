Put forth as a potential Democratic vice presidential nominee in the 1992 U.S. presidential election[1] or even potentially replacing Vice President Dan Quayle as the Republican vice presidential nominee,[2] Powell eventually declared himself a Republican and began to campaign for Republican candidates in 1995.[3][4] He was touted as a possible opponent of Bill Clinton in the 1996 U.S. presidential election, possibly capitalizing on a split conservative vote in Iowa[5] and even leading New Hampshire polls for the GOP nomination,[6] but Powell declined, citing a lack of passion for politics.[7] Powell defeated Clinton 50â€“38 in a hypothetical match-up proposed to voters in the exit polls conducted on Election Day.[8] Despite not standing in the race, Powell won the Republican New Hampshire Vice-Presidential primary on write-in votes.[9]
In 1997, Powell founded America's Promise with the objective of helping children from all socioeconomic sectors. That same year saw the establishment of The Colin L. Powell Center for Leadership and Service.
