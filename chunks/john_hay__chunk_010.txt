They remained at the White House pending the arrival and training of replacements.[22]
Hay did not accompany the Lincolns to Ford's Theatre on the night of April 14, 1865, but remained at the White House, drinking whiskey with Robert Lincoln. When the two were informed that the President had been shot, they hastened to the Petersen House, a boarding house where the stricken Lincoln had been taken. Hay remained by Lincoln's deathbed through the night[23] and was present when he died. At the moment of Lincoln's death, Hay observed "a look of unspeakable peace came upon his worn features".[24] He heard War Secretary Edwin Stanton's declaration, "Now he belongs to the ages."[25]
