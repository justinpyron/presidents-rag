The tension over Nicaragua soon abated, but the May 1895 death of Secretary Gresham, who like Bayard had favored cooperation with the British, led to increased disagreement over the Venezuela issue.[9] Cleveland appointed Richard Olney to take over the State Department, and Olney soon proved more confrontational than his predecessor.
