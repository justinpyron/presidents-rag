Beliefs
From a young age, Carter showed a deep commitment to evangelical Christianity.[15][16] Carter taught Sunday school at Maranatha Baptist Church in Plains, Georgia.[17] At a private inauguration worship service, the preacher was Nelson Price, the pastor of Roswell Street Baptist Church of Marietta, Georgia.[18] An evangelical Christian, Carter appealed to voters after the scandals of the Nixon Administration, and is credited with popularizing the term "born again" into American lexicon during the 1976 American presidential campaign.[16][19][20][21] As president, Carter prayed several times a day, and professed that Jesus was the driving force in his life.
