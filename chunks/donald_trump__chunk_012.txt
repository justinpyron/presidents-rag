Trump had cameos in many films and television shows from 1985 to 2001.[3]
Starting in the 1990s, Trump was a guest about 24 times on the nationally syndicated Howard Stern Show.[4] He had his own short-form talk radio program called Trumped! from 2004 to 2008.[5][6] From 2011 until 2015, he was a guest commentator on Fox & Friends.[7][8]
From 2004 to 2015, Trump was co-producer and host of reality shows The Apprentice and The Celebrity Apprentice. On the shows, Trump was a superrich and successful chief executive who eliminated contestants with the catchphrase "you're fired". The New York Times called his portrayal a "highly flattering, highly fictionalized version of Mr. Trump". The shows remade his image for millions of viewers nationwide.[9][10] With the related licensing agreements, they earned him more than $400Â million.[11]
In February 2021, Trump, who had been a member of SAG-AFTRA since 1989, resigned to avoid a disciplinary hearing regarding the January 6 attack.[12] Two days later, the union permanently barred him.[13]
