On July 29, 1994, the Clinton administration launched the first official White House website, whitehouse.gov.[39] The site was followed with three more versions, with the final version being launched on July 21, 2000.[39] The White House website was part of a wider movement of the Clinton administration toward web-based communication. According to Robert Longley, "Clinton and Gore were responsible for pressing almost all federal agencies, the U.S. court system and the U.S. military onto the Internet, thus opening up America's government to more of America's citizens than ever before.
