The Soviet Union's success in exploding an atomic weapon in 1949 and the fall of the nationalist Chinese the same year led many Americans to conclude subversion by Soviet spies was responsible and to demand that communists be rooted out from the government and other places of influence.[117][118] Hoping to contain these fears, Truman began a "loyalty program" with Executive Order 9835 in 1947.[119] However, Truman got himself into deeper trouble when he called the Hiss trial a "red herring".[120][121] Wisconsin Senator Joseph McCarthy accused the State Department of harboring communists and rode the controversy to political fame,[122] leading to the Second Red Scare,[123] also known as McCarthyism. McCarthy's stifling accusations made it difficult to speak out against him. This led Truman to call McCarthy "the greatest asset the Kremlin has" by "torpedo[ing] the bipartisan foreign policy of the United States."[124]
