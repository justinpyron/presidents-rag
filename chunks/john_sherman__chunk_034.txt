Hayes issued an executive order that forbade federal office holders from being required to make campaign contributions or otherwise taking part in party politics.[18] Chester A. Arthur, the Collector of the Port of New York, and his subordinates Alonzo B. Cornell and George H. Sharpe, all Conkling supporters, refused to obey the president's order.[18] Sherman agreed with Hayes that the three had to resign, but he made clear in a letter to Arthur that he had no personal grudge against the Collector.[19] In September 1877, Hayes demanded the three men's resignations, which they refused to give.[20] He submitted appointments to the Senate for confirmation as their replacements but the Senate's Commerce Committee, which Conkling chaired, voted unanimously to reject the nominees.[20]
