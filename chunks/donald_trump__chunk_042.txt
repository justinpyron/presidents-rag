To prevent another imminent shutdown in February 2019, Congress passed and Trump signed a funding bill that included $1.375 billion for 55 miles (89 km) of bollard border fencing.[115] Trump also declared a national emergency on the southern border, intending to divert $6.1 billion of funds Congress had allocated to other purposes.[115] Trump vetoed a joint resolution to overturn the declaration, and the Senate voted against a veto override.[116] Legal challenges to the diversion of $2.5 billion originally meant for the Department of Defense's drug interdiction efforts[117][118] and $3.6 billion originally meant for military construction[119][120] were unsuccessful.
