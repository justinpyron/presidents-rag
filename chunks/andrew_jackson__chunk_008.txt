The bullet hit Jackson in the chest, but shattered against his breastbone.[44] He returned fire and killed Dickinson. The killing tarnished Jackson's reputation.[45]
Later that year, Jackson became involved in former vice president Aaron Burr's plan to conquer Spanish Florida and drive the Spanish from Texas. Burr, who was touring what was then the Western United States after mortally wounding Alexander Hamilton in a duel, stayed with the Jacksons at the Hermitage in 1805.[46] He eventually persuaded Jackson to join his adventure. In October 1806, Jackson wrote James Winchester that the United States "can conquer not only [Florida], but all Spanish North America".[47] He informed the Tennessee militia that it should be ready to march at a moment's notice "when the government and constituted authority of our country require it",[48] and agreed to provide boats and provisions for the expedition.[46] Jackson sent a letter to President Thomas Jefferson telling him that Tennessee was ready to defend the nation's honor.[49]
Jackson also expressed uncertainty about the enterprise.
