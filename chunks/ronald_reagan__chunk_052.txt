Support for Brady Bill
Reagan publicly favored the Brady Bill, drawing criticism from gun control opponents.[6] In 1989, in his first public appearance after leaving office and shortly after the Stockton schoolyard shooting at Cleveland Elementary School in Stockton, California, he stated: "I do not believe in taking away the right of the citizen to own guns for sporting, for hunting, and so forth, or for home defense. But I do believe that an AK-47, a machine gun, is not a sporting weapon or needed for the defense of the home".[7][8]
In March 1991, Reagan wrote an op-ed in the New York Times, titled "Why I'm for the Brady Bill".[9][10] In May 1994, Reagan, Gerald Ford, and Jimmy Carter sent a letter to House members, urging them to support the controversial Federal Assault Weapons Ban.[11]
