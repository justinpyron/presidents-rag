She founded the school's international relations club and was its first president.[21] She attended Wellesley College, in Wellesley, Massachusetts, on a full scholarship, majoring in political science, and graduated in 1959.[22] The topic of her senior thesis was Zdeněk Fierlinger, a former Czechoslovakian prime minister.[23] She became a naturalized U.S. citizen in 1957, and joined the College Democrats of America.[24]
While home in Denver from Wellesley, Korbel worked as an intern for The Denver Post. There she met Joseph Albright. He was the nephew of Alicia Patterson, owner of Newsday and wife of philanthropist Harry Frank Guggenheim.[25] Korbel converted to the Episcopal Church at the time of her marriage.[7][8] The couple were married in Wellesley in 1959, shortly after her graduation.[22] They lived in Rolla, Missouri, while Joseph completed his military service at nearby Fort Leonard Wood. During this time, Albright worked at The Rolla Daily News.[26]
The couple moved to Joseph's hometown of Chicago, Illinois, in January 1960. Joseph worked at the Chicago Sun-Times as a journalist, and Albright worked as a picture editor for Encyclopædia Britannica.[27] The following year, Joseph Albright began work at Newsday in New York City, and the couple moved to Garden City on Long Island.[28] That year, she gave birth to twin daughters, Alice Patterson Albright and Anne Korbel Albright.
