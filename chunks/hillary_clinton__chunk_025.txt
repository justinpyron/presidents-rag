The book spent 18 weeks on the New York Times Best Seller List that year, including three weeks at number one.[43] By 2000, it had sold 450,000 copies in hardcover and another 200,000 in paperback.[44] Clinton received the Grammy Award for Best Spoken Word Album in 1997 for the book's audio recording.[45]
Other books published by Clinton when she was the first lady include Dear Socks, Dear Buddy: Kids' Letters to the First Pets (1998) and An Invitation to the White House: At Home with History (2000). In 2001, she wrote an afterword to the children's book Beatrice's Goat.[46]
Clinton also published a weekly syndicated newspaper column titled "Talking It Over" from 1995 to 2000.[47][48] It focused on her experiences and those of women, children and families she met during her travels around the world.[49]
