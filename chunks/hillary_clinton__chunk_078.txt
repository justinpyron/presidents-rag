Basing her economic philosophy on inclusive capitalism, Clinton proposed a "clawback" that would rescind tax relief and other benefits for companies that move jobs overseas; providing incentives for companies that share profits with employees, communities and the environment, rather than focusing on short-term profits to increase stock value and rewarding shareholders; increasing collective bargaining rights; and placing an "exit tax" on companies that move their headquarters out of America to pay a lower tax rate overseas.[7]
Domestic policy
Clinton accepts the scientific consensus on climate change and supports cap-and-trade,[8] and opposed the Keystone XL pipeline.[9] She supported "equal pay for equal work", to address current shortfalls in how much women are paid to do the same jobs men do.[10] Clinton has explicitly focused on family issues and supports universal preschool.[11] These programs would be funded by proposing tax increases on the wealthy, including a "fair share surcharge".[12] Clinton supported the Affordable Care Act[13] and would have added a "public option" that competed with private insurers and enabled people "50 or 55 and up" to buy into Medicare.[14][15]
