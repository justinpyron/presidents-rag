During a June 2007 visit to the predominantly Muslim[305] Albania, Bush was greeted enthusiastically. Albania has a population of 2.8 million,[306] has troops in both Iraq and Afghanistan, and the country's government is highly supportive of American foreign policy.[307] A huge image of the President was hung in the middle of the capital city of Tirana flanked by Albanian and American flags while a local street was named after him.[308][309] A shirt-sleeved statue of Bush was unveiled in Fushë-Krujë, a few kilometers northwest of Tirana.[310] The Bush administration's support for the unilateral declaration of independence of Albanian-majority Kosovo, while endearing him to the Albanians, troubled U.S. relations with Serbia, leading to the February 2008 torching of the U.S. embassy in Belgrade.[311]
Post-presidency (2009–present)
Residence
