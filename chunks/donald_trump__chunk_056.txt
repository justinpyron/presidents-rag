On April 22, Trump signed an executive order restricting some forms of immigration.[244] In late spring and early summer, with infections and deaths continuing to rise, he adopted a strategy of blaming the states rather than accepting that his initial assessments of the pandemic were overly optimistic or his failure to provide presidential leadership.[245]
White House Coronavirus Task Force
Trump established the White House Coronavirus Task Force on January 29, 2020.[246] Beginning in mid-March, Trump held a daily task force press conference, joined by medical experts and other administration officials,[247] sometimes disagreeing with them by promoting unproven treatments.[248] Trump was the main speaker at the briefings, where he praised his own response to the pandemic, frequently criticized rival presidential candidate Joe Biden, and denounced the press.[247][249] On March 16, he acknowledged for the first time that the pandemic was not under control and that months of disruption to daily lives and a recession might occur.[250] His repeated use of "Chinese virus" and "China virus" to describe COVID-19 drew criticism from health experts.[251][252][253]
