That summer, Garfield suffered from jaundice and significant weight loss.[a][18] He was forced to return home, where his wife nursed him back to health.[18] While he was home, Garfield's friends worked to gain him the Republican nomination for Congress, but he refused to campaign with the delegates.[19] He returned to military duty that autumn and went to Washington to await his next assignment.[20] During this period of idleness, a rumor of an extramarital affair caused friction in the Garfields' marriage until Lucretia eventually chose to overlook it.[21] Garfield repeatedly received tentative assignments that were quickly withdrawn, to his frustration.[22] In the meantime, he served on the court-martial of Fitz John Porter for his tardiness at the Second Battle of Bull Run.[23] He was convinced of Porter's guilt and voted with his fellow generals to convict Porter.[23] The trial lasted almost two month
