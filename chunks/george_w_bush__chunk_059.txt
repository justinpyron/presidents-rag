Job approval
Bush began his presidency with approval ratings near 60 percent.[255] After the September 11 attacks, Bush gained an approval rating of 90 percent,[256] maintaining 80–90 percent approval for four months after the attacks. It remained over 50 percent during most of his first term[257] and then fell to as low as 19 percent in his second term.[258]
In 2000 and again in 2004, Time magazine named George W. Bush as its Person of the Year, a title awarded to someone who the editors believe "has done the most to influence the events of the year".[259] In May 2004, Gallup reported that 89 percent of the Republican electorate approved of Bush.[260] However, the support waned due mostly to a minority of Republicans' frustration with him on issues of spending, illegal immigration, and Middle Eastern affairs.[261]
