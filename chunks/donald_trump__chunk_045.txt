Asia
China, Hong Kong, Taiwan
See also: China–United States relations § First Trump administration (2017–2021)
Trump repeatedly accused China of taking unfair advantage of the U.S.[146] He launched a trade war against China that was widely characterized as a failure,[147][148] sanctioned Huawei for alleged ties to Iran,[149] significantly increased visa restrictions on Chinese students and scholars,[150] and classified China as a currency manipulator.[151] Trump also juxtaposed verbal attacks on China with praise of Chinese Communist Party leader Xi Jinping,[152] which was attributed to trade war negotiations.[153] After initially praising China for its handling of COVID-19,[154] he began a campaign of criticism starting in March 2020.[155]
Trump said he resisted punishing China for its human rights abuses against ethnic minorities in the Xinjiang region for fear of jeopardizing trade negotiations.[156] In July 2020, the Trump administration imposed sanctions and visa restrictions against senior Chinese officials, in response to expanded mass detention camps holding more than a million of the country's Uyghur minority.[157]
