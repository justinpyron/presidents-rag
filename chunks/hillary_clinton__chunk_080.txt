Foreign policy
On foreign affairs, Clinton voted in favor of the Authorization for Use of Military Force Against Iraq in October 2002,[24] a vote she later said she regretted.[25] She favored arming Syria's rebel fighters in 2012 and has called for the removal of Syrian president Bashar al-Assad.[26] She supported the NATO bombing of Yugoslavia in 1999 and the NATO-led military intervention in Libya to oust former Libyan leader Muammar Gaddafi in 2011.[27][28] Clinton is in favor of maintaining American influence in the Middle East.[23] She has told the American Israel Public Affairs Committee, "America can't ever be neutral when it comes to Israel's security and survival."[29] Clinton expressed support for Israel's right to defend itself during the 2006 Lebanon War and 2014 Israelâ€“Gaza conflict.[30][31] In a 2017 interview, after a poison gas attack in Syria, Clinton said that she had favored more aggressive action against Bashar al-Assad: "I think we should have been more willing to confront Assad.
