Memory and memorials
Main article: Memorials to Abraham Lincoln
Lincoln's portrait appears on two denominations of United States currency, the penny and the $5 bill. He appears on postage stamps across the world.[50][51] While he is usually portrayed bearded, he did not grow a beard until 1860 at the suggestion of 11-year-old Grace Bedell. He was the first of five presidents to do so.[52]
He has been memorialized in many town, city, and county names,[53] including the capital of Nebraska.[54] The United States Navy Nimitz-class aircraft carrier USS Abraham Lincoln (CVN-72) is named after Lincoln, the second Navy ship to bear his name.[55] The Lincoln Memorial is one of the most visited monuments in the nation's capital[56] and is one of the most visited National Park Service sites in the country.[57] Ford's Theatre, among the most visited sites in Washington, D.C.,[57] is across the street from Petersen House, where Lincoln died.[58] Memorials in Springfield, Illinois, include the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum, Lincoln's home, and his tomb.[59] A portrait carving of Lincoln appears with those of three other presidents on Mount Rushmore, which receives about 3 million visitors a year.[60] An influential statue of Lincoln stands in Lincol
