Trump's last major construction project was the 92-story mixed-use Trump International Hotel and Tower (Chicago) which opened in 2008. In 2024, the New York Times and ProPublica reported that the Internal Revenue Service was investigating whether Trump had twice written off losses incurred through construction cost overruns and lagging sales of residential units in the building Trump had declared to be worthless on his 2008 tax return.[18][19]
Atlantic City casinos
In 1984, Trump opened Harrah's at Trump Plaza, a hotel and casino, with financing and management help from the Holiday Corporation.[20] It was unprofitable, and Trump paid Holiday $70 million in May 1986 to take sole control.[21] In 1985, Trump bought the unopened Atlantic City Hilton Hotel and renamed it Trump Castle.[22] Both casinos filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in 1992.[23]
Trump bought a third Atlantic City venue in 1988, the Trump Taj Mahal. It was financed with $675 million in junk bonds and completed for $1.1 billion, opening in April 1990.[24][25] Trump filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in 1991.
