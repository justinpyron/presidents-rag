Reagan won an upset victory, convincing party delegates that Ford's nomination was no longer guaranteed.[14] Reagan won subsequent victories in Texas, Alabama, Georgia, and Indiana with his attacks on social programs, opposition to forced busing, increased support from inclined voters of a declining George Wallace campaign for the Democratic nomination,[15] and repeated criticisms of Ford and Kissinger's policies, including détente.[16]
The result was a seesaw battle for the 1,130 delegates required for their party's nomination that neither would reach before the Kansas City convention[17] in August[18] and Ford replacing mentions of détente with Reagan's preferred phrase, "peace through strength".[19] Reagan took John Sears' advice of choosing liberal Richard Schweiker as his running mate, hoping to pry loose of delegates from Pennsylvania and other states,[20] and distract Ford.
