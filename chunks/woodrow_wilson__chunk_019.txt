Wilson engaged in a spirited campaign, criss-crossing the country to deliver numerous speeches.[18] Ultimately, he took 42 percent of the popular vote and 435 of the 531 electoral votes.[19] Roosevelt won most of the remaining electoral votes and 27.4 percent of the popular vote, one of the strongest third party performances in U.S. history. Taft won 23.2 percent of the popular vote but just 8 electoral votes, while Debs won 6 percent of the popular vote.
