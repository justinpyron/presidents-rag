Secretary of the Treasury
Sherman's financial expertise and his friendship with Hayes made him a natural choice for Treasury Secretary in 1877.[1] Like Grant before him, Hayes had not consulted party leaders about his cabinet appointments, and the Senate took the then-unusual step of referring all of them to committee.[2] Two days later, senators approved Sherman's nomination after an hour of debate, and he began lobbying his former colleagues to approve the other nominations, which they eventually did.[2] Hayes and Sherman became close friends in the next four years, taking regular carriage rides together to discuss matters of state in private.[3] In the Treasury, as in the Senate, Sherman was confronted with two tasks: first, to prepare for specie resumption when it took effect in 1879; second, to deal with the backlash against the diminution of silver coinage.
