Garfield's hunch was correct.[31] Consequently, his ride became legendary and Rosecrans's error reignited criticism about the latter's leadership.[31] While Rosecrans's army had avoided disaster, they were stranded in Chattanooga, surrounded by Bragg's army. Garfield sent a telegram to Secretary of War Edwin M. Stanton alerting Washington to the need for reinforcements to avoid annihilation. Lincoln and Halleck responded to the request for reinforcements by sending 20,000 troops to Garfield by rail within nine days.[32] In the meantime, Grant was promoted to command of the western armies and quickly replaced Rosecrans with George H. Thomas.[33] Garfield was ordered to report to Washington, where he was promoted to major general.[34] According to historian Jean Edward Smith, Grant and Garfield had a "guarded relationship" since Grant promoted Thomas, rather than Garfield, to command of the Army of the Cumberland after Rosecrans's dismissal.[35]
