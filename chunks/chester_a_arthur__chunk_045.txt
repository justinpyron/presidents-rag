Health and travel
Shortly after becoming president, Arthur was diagnosed with Bright's disease, a kidney ailment now referred to as nephritis.[58] He attempted to keep his condition private, but by 1883 rumors of his illness began to circulate; he had become thinner and more aged in appearance, and struggled to keep the pace of the presidency.[58] To rejuvenate his health outside the confines of Washington, Arthur and some political friends traveled to Florida in April 1883.[59] The vacation had the opposite effect, and Arthur suffered from intense pain before returning to Washington.[59] Later that year, on the advice of Missouri Senator George Graham Vest, he visited Yellowstone National Park.[60] Reporters accompanied the presidential party, helping to publicize the new National Park system.[60] The
