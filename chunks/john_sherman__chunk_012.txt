Financing the Civil War
The Civil War expenditures quickly strained the government's already fragile financial situation and Sherman, assigned to the Senate Finance Committee, was involved in the process of increasing the revenue.[6] In July 1861, Congress authorized the government to issue Demand Notes, the first form of paper money issued directly by the United States government.[7] The notes were redeemable in specie (i.e., gold or silver coin) but, as Sherman would note in his memoirs, they did not solve the revenue problem, as the government did not have the coin to redeem the notes should they all be presented for payment.[8] To solve this problem, Chase asked for and Congress authorized the issuance of $150 million in bonds, which (as banks purchased them with gold) replenished the treasury.[7] Congress also sought to increase revenue when they passed the Revenue Act of 1861, which imposed the first federal income tax in American history.
