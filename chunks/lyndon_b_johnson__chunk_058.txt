Taylor reported that the bombing offensive against North Vietnam had been ineffective and that the South Vietnamese army was outclassed and in danger of collapse.[143] In late July, McNamara and Johnson's top advisors recommended an increase in U.S. soldiers from 75,000 to over 200,000.[144] Johnson agreed but felt boxed in by unpalatable choices. If he sent additional troops he would be attacked as an interventionist, and if he did not, he thought he risked being impeached.[145] By October 1965, there were over 200,000 troops deployed in Vietnam.[146] Throughout 1965, few members of Congress or the administration openly criticized Johnson's handling of the war.[147]
