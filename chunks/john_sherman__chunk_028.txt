Election of 1876
After the close of the session, Sherman returned to Ohio to campaign for the Republican nominee for governor there, former governor Rutherford B. Hayes.[69] The issue of specie payments was debated in the campaign, with Hayes endorsing Sherman's position and his Democratic opponent, incumbent Governor William Allen, in favor of increased circulation of greenbacks redeemable in bonds.[69] Hayes won a narrow victory and was soon mentioned as a possible presidential candidate in 1876.[70] The controversy over resumption carried into the presidential election.
