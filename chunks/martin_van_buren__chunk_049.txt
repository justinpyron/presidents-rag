Election of 1848
Though he had previously helped maintain a balance between the Barnburners and Hunkers, the two factions of the New York Democratic Party, Van Buren moved closer to the Barnburners after the 1844 Democratic National Convention.[25] The split in the state party worsened during Polk's presidency, as his administration lavished patronage on the Hunkers.[26] In his retirement, Van Buren also grew increasingly opposed to slavery.[27]
