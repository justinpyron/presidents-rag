Native Americans
Lincoln's relationship with Native Americans started before he was born, with their killing of his grandfather in front of his sons, including Lincoln's father Thomas.[117] Lincoln himself served as a captain in the state militia during the Black Hawk War but saw no combat.[118] Lincoln used appointments to the Indian Bureau as a reward to supporters from Minnesota and Wisconsin. While in office his administration faced difficulties guarding Western settlers, railroads, and telegraphs, from Indian attacks.[119]
On August 17, 1862, the Dakota War broke out in Minnesota.
