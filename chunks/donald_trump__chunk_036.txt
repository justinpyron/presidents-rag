Immigration
Main article: Immigration policy of Donald Trump
Trump's proposed immigration policies were a topic of bitter debate during the 2016 campaign. He promised to build a wall on the Mexico–U.S. border to restrict illegal movement and vowed that Mexico would pay for it.[72] He pledged to deport millions of illegal immigrants residing in the U.S.,[73] and criticized birthright citizenship for incentivizing "anchor babies".[74] As president, he frequently described illegal immigration as an "invasion" and conflated immigrants with the criminal gang MS-13.[75]
Trump drastically escalated immigration enforcement, including implementing harsher immigration enforcement policies against asylum seekers from Central America than any modern U.S. president.[76][77]
