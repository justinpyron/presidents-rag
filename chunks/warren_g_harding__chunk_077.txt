Child
Harding fathered Elizabeth Ann Blaesing out of wedlock with his mistress Nan Britton. In 2015, DNA testing proved Harding was Blaesing's father. Harding had vigorously denied paternity. According to family lore, Harding was infertile and could not have fathered a child, having suffered from mumps in childhood,[7] and his siblings, nieces, and nephews maintained this position for 90 years.[7]
In 1927, Britton, who was also from Marion, published The President's Daughter, writing that Harding had fathered her child Elizabeth Ann Blaesing. The book, which was dedicated to "all unwedded mothers" and "their innocent children whose fathers are usually not known to the world", was sold, like pornography, door-to-door, wrapped in brown paper.[8] Harding's reputation had deteriorated since his death in 1923, and many believed Britton.[9] The public was tantalized by salacious details such as Britton's claim that the two had sex in a closet near the Oval Office, with Secret Service agents posted to ward off intruders.[9]
Despite being widely believed, Britton lost a libel lawsuit against a rebuttal of her book.[10]
Britton said that Harding had provided child support of $500 per month for the daughter he never met,[11] but that she had destroyed romantic correspondence from him at his request.[7][11]
