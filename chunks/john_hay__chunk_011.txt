Early diplomatic career
Hay sailed for Paris at the end of June 1865.[1] There, he served under U.S. Minister to France John Bigelow.[2] The workload was not heavy, and Hay found time to enjoy the pleasures of Paris.[3] When Bigelow resigned in mid-1866,[4] Hay, as was customary, submitted his resignation, though he was asked to remain until Bigelow's successor was in place, and stayed until January 1867. He consulted with Secretary of State Seward, asking him for "anything worth having".[5] Seward suggested the post of Minister to Sweden, but reckoned without the new president, Andrew Johnson, who had his own candidate. Seward offered Hay a job as his private secretary, but Hay declined, and returned home to Warsaw, Illinois.[6]
Initially happy to be home, Hay quickly grew restive,[7] and he was glad to hear, in early June 1867, that he had been appointed secretary of legation to act as chargé d'affaires at Vienna.
