Reforms and rotation in office

Jackson believed that Adams's administration had been corrupt and he initiated investigations into all executive departments.[7] These investigations revealed that $280,000 (equivalent to $8,000,000 in 2023) was stolen from the Treasury. Jackson implemented a principle he called "rotation in office". The previous custom had been for the president to leave the existing appointees in office, replacing them through attrition.
