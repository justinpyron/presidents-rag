Carter endorsed Vice President Al Gore days before the 2000 presidential election,[73] and in subsequent years voiced his opinion that Gore won the election,[74] despite Bush's eventual victory following the Supreme Court's ruling in Bush v. Gore.[75]
In the 2004 presidential election, Carter endorsed John Kerry and spoke at the 2004 Democratic National Convention.[76] He also voiced concern about another voting mishap in Florida.[77]
During the 2008 Democratic presidential primaries, it was speculated that Carter would endorse Barack Obama over his main primary rival Hillary Clinton, as Carter spoke favorably of Obama, as did other members of the Carter family.[78][79] Carter also commented on Clinton ending her bid when superdelegates voted after the June 3 primary.[80] Carter criticized the Republican nominee, John McCain.[81][82] Carter warned Obama against selecting Clinton as his running mate.[83]
