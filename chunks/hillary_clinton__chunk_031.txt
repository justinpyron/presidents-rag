First term
Because Bill Clinton's term as president did not end until 17 days after she was sworn in, upon entering the Senate, Clinton became the first and so far only first lady to serve as a senator and first lady concurrently. Clinton maintained a low public profile and built relationships with senators from both parties when she started her term.[10] She forged alliances with religiously inclined senators by becoming a regular participant in the Senate Prayer Breakfast.[11][12] She sat on five Senate committees: Committee on Budget (2001–02),[13] Committee on Armed Services (2003–09),[14] Committee on Environment and Public Works (2001–09), Committee on Health, Education, Labor and Pensions (2001–09)[13] and Special Committee on Aging.[15]
She was also a member of the Commission on Security and Cooperation in Europe[16] (2001–09).[17]
Following the September 11 terrorist attacks, Clinton sought to obtain funding for the recovery efforts in New York City and security improvements in her state. Working with New York's senior senator, Chuck Schumer, she was instrumental in securing $21 billion in funding for the World Trade Center site's redevelopment.[18] She subsequently took a leading role in investigating the health issues faced by 9/11 first responders.[19] Clinton voted for the USA Patriot Act in October 2001.
