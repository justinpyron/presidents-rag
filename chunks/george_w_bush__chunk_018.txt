2008 financial crisis
In December 2007, the United States entered the longest post–World War II recession,[4] caused by a housing market correction, a subprime mortgage crisis, soaring oil prices, and other factors. In February 2008, 63,000 jobs were lost, a five-year record,[31] and in November, over 500,000 jobs were lost, which marked the largest loss of jobs in the United States in 34 years.[32] The Bureau of Labor Statistics reported that in the last four months of 2008, 1.9 million jobs were lost.[33] By the end of 2008, the U.S. had lost 2.6 million jobs.[34]
