Marriage and family

In 1883, Wilson met and fell in love with Ellen Louise Axson.[1] He proposed marriage in September 1883; she accepted, but they agreed to postpone marriage while Wilson attended graduate school.[2] Axson graduated from Art Students League of New York, worked in portraiture, and received a medal for one of her works from the Exposition Universelle (1878) in Paris.[3] She agreed to sacrifice further independent artistic pursuits in order to marry Wilson in 1885.[4] Ellen learned German so she could help translate German-language political science publications relevant to Woodrow's research.[5]
