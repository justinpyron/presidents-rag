Philadelphia's law libraries gave Blaine the chance to, at last, begin to study the law, but in 1853 he received a more tempting offer: to become editor and co-owner of the Kennebec Journal.[16] Blaine had spent several vacations in his wife's native state of Maine and had become friendly with the Journal's editors.
