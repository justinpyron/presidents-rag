Obama's most significant accomplishment is generally considered to be the Affordable Care Act (ACA), provisions of which went into effect from 2010 to 2020. Many attempts by Senate Republicans to repeal the ACA, including a "skinny repeal", have thus far failed.[7] However, in 2017, the penalty for violating the individual mandate was repealed effective 2019.[8] Together with the Health Care and Education Reconciliation Act amendment, it represents the U.S. healthcare system's most significant regulatory overhaul and expansion of coverage since the passage of Medicare and Medicaid in 1965.[9][10][11][12]
Many commentators credit Obama with averting a threatened depression and pulling the economy back from the Great Recession.[7] According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the Obama administration created 11.3 million jobs from the month after his first inauguration to the end of his second term.[13] In 2010, Obama signed into effect the Dodd–Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act.
