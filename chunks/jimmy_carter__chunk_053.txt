Carter announced his reelection campaign in December 1979.[156] A month earlier, Senator Ted Kennedy had announced his candidacy for the Democratic nomination.[157] During the Democratic presidential primaries, questions about Kennedy were a frequent subject of Carter's press conferences.[158][159] Despite winning key states such as California and New York, Kennedy surprised his supporters by running a weak campaign. Carter won most of the primaries and secured renomination. He later wrote that the strongest opposition to his policies came from the Democratic Party's liberal wing, which he attributed to Kennedy's ambition to replace him as president.[160] Kennedy had mobilized the liberal wing, which weakened Carter's support in the general election.[161]
