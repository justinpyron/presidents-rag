Until 2018, the media rarely referred to Trump's falsehoods as lies, including when he repeated demonstrably false statements.[17][18][19]
In 2020, Trump was a significant source of disinformation on mail-in voting and the COVID-19 pandemic.[20][21] His attacks on mail-in ballots and other election practices weakened public faith in the integrity of the 2020 presidential election,[22][23] while his disinformation about the pandemic delayed and weakened the national response to it.[24][20]
Rhetoric
