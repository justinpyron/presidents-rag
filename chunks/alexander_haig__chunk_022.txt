Later life, health, and death

In 1980 Haig had a double heart bypass operation.[1]
After leaving the Reagan White House, Haig took a seat on the MGM board of directors in an effort to cultivate a film career.[2] He supervised the development of John Milius' Red Dawn (1984) and made significant changes to it.[3] While heading a consulting firm in the 1980s and 1990s, he served as a director for various struggling businesses, including computer manufacturer Commodore International.[4] He also served as a founding corporate director of America Online.[5]
