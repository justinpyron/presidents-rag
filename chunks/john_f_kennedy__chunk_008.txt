Seven days after the collision, with the coconut message delivered, the PT-109 crew were rescued.[16][17]
Almost immediately, the PT-109 rescue became a highly publicized event. The story was chronicled by John Hersey in The New Yorker in 1944 (decades later it was the basis of a successful film).[17] It followed Kennedy into politics and provided a strong foundation for his appeal as a leader.[18] Hersey portrayed Kennedy as a modest, self-deprecating hero.[19] For his courage and leadership, Kennedy was awarded the Navy and Marine Corps Medal, and the injuries he suffered during the incident qualified him for a Purple Heart.[18]
