The change in guidelines was made by HHS political appointees under Trump administration pressure, against the wishes of CDC scientists.[277][278] The day after this political interference was reported, the testing guideline was changed back to its original recommendation.[278]
Despite record numbers of COVID-19 cases in the U.S. from mid-June onward and an increasing percentage of positive test results, Trump largely continued to downplay the pandemic, including his false claim in early July 2020 that 99 percent of COVID-19 cases are "totally harmless".[279][280] He began insisting that all states should resume in-person education in the fall despite a July spike in reported cases.[281]
