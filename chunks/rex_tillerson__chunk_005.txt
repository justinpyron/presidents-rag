Tillerson was a friend of Igor Sechin,[16][17][18] the executive chairman of Rosneft, the Russian state oil company, and leader of the Kremlin's Siloviki (security/military) faction,[19][better source needed] who has been described as "Russia's second-most powerful person" after Putin.[20] Exxon owns a dacha next door to Sechin's, and Tillerson would often visit there.[2] Tillerson led Sechin on a tour of New York City, dining on caviar with him and Putin at Per Se restaurant.[2] In 2006, Exxon avoided making any government concessions in its Sakhalin-I oil field after Royal Dutch Shell was forced to sell half of its ownership in Sakhalin-II by the Russian government.[2]
