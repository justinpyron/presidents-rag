Trump was the only president never to reach a 50 percent approval rating in the Gallup poll, which dates to 1938. His approval ratings showed a record-high partisan gap: 88 percent among Republicans and 7 percent among Democrats.[1] Until September 2020, the ratings were unusually stable, reaching a high of 49 percent and a low of 35 percent.[2] Trump finished his term with an approval rating between 29 and 34 percent—the lowest of any president since modern polling began—and a record-low average of 41 percent throughout his presidency.[1][3]
In Gallup's annual poll asking Americans to name the man they admire the most, Trump placed second to Obama in 2017 and 2018, tied with Obama for first in 2019, and placed first in 2020.[4][5] Since Gallup started conducting the poll in 1948, Trump is the first elected president not to be named most admired in his first year in office.[6]
A Gallup poll in 134 countries comparing the approval ratings of U.S. leadership between 2016 and 2017 found that Trump led Obama in job approval in only 29 countries, most of them non-democracies;[7] approval of U.S. leadership plummeted among allies and G7 countries.
