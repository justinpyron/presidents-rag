In late 2016, Time named Trump its Person of the Year.[59][60]
Trump's election victory sparked protests in major U.S. cities.[61][62] On the day after his inauguration, an estimated 2.6 million people worldwide, including an estimated half million in Washington, D.C., protested against him in the Women's Marches.[63]
2020 presidential campaign
Main article: Donald Trump 2020 presidential campaign
See also: 2020 United States presidential debates
Breaking with precedent, Trump filed to run for a second term within a few hours of assuming the presidency.[64] He held his first reelection rally less than a month after taking office[65] and officially became the Republican nominee in August 2020.[66]
In his first two years in office, Trump's reelection committee reported raising $67.5 million and began 2019 with $19.3 million in cash.[67] By July 2020, the Trump campaign and the Republican Party had raised $1.1 billion and spent $800 million, losing their cash advantage over Biden.[68] The cash shortage forced the campaign to scale back advertising spending.[69]
Trump campaign advertisements focused on crime, claiming that cities would descend into lawlessness if Biden won.[70] Trump repeatedly misrepresented Biden's positions[71][72] and shifted to appeals to racism.[73]
