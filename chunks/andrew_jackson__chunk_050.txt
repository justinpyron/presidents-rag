During the conflict, Jackson covertly allowed the settlers to obtain weapons and money from the United States.[146] They defeated the Mexican military in April 1836 and declared the region an independent country, the Republic of Texas. The new Republic asked Jackson to recognize and annex it. Although Jackson wanted to do so, he was hesitant because he was unsure it could maintain independence from Mexico.[139] He also was concerned because Texas had legalized slavery, which was an issue that could divide the Democrats during the 1836 election. Jackson recognized the Republic of Texas on the last full day of his presidency, March 3, 1837.[147]
Judiciary
Further information: List of federal judges appointed by Andrew Jackson
Jackson appointed six justices to the Supreme Court.[148] Most were undistinguished. Jackson nominated Roger B. Taney in January 1835 to the Court in reward for his services, but the nomination failed to win Senate approval.[149]
When Chief Justice Marshall died in 1835, Jackson again nominated Taney for Chief Justice; he was confirmed by the new Senate,[150] serving as Chief Justice until 1864.[151] He was regarded with respect during his career on the bench, but he is most remembered for his widely condemned decision in Dred Scott v.
