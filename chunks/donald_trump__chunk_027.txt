Beginning with his 2016 campaign, Trump's politics and rhetoric led to the creation of a political movement known as Trumpism. Trump's political base has been compared to a cult of personality.[b]
On July 13, 2024, Trump's ear was grazed by a bullet[160] in an assassination attempt at a campaign rally in Butler Township, Pennsylvania.[161][162] The campaign declined to disclose medical records.[163] Two days later, the 2024 Republican National Convention nominated Trump as their presidential candidate, with Senator JD Vance as his running mate.[164]
Election and presidential transition
Main articles: 2024 United States presidential election and Second presidential transition of Donald Trump
See also: Second presidency of Donald Trump
Trump was elected the 47th president of the United States in November 2024, defeating the incumbent vice president Kamala Harris.[165] He became the second president in U.S. history elected to serve nonconsecutive terms after former president Grover Cleveland, who won reelection in 1892.[166] The Associated Press and BBC News described it as an extraordinary comeback for a former president.[167][168][169][170]
