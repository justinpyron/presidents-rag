In the aftermath, a Moscow–Washington hotline was established to ensure clear communications between the leaders of the two countries.[73] This crisis brought the world closer to nuclear war than at any point before or after, but "the humanity" of Khrushchev and Kennedy prevailed.[74] The crisis improved the image of American willpower and the president's credibility. Kennedy's approval rating increased from 66% to 77% immediately thereafter.[75]
Latin America and communism
Further information: Presidency of John F. Kennedy § Latin America
See also: Alliance for Progress
Believing that "those who make peaceful revolution impossible, will make violent revolution inevitable,"[76][77] Kennedy sought to contain the perceived threat of communism in Latin America by establishing the Alliance for Progress, which sent aid to some countries and sought greater human rights standards in the region.[78] In response to Kennedy's plea, Congress voted for an initial grant of $500 million in May 1961.[79] The Alliance for Progress supported the construction of housing, schools, airports, hospitals, clinics and water-purification projects as well as the distribution of free textbooks to students.[80] However, the program did not meet many of its goals.
