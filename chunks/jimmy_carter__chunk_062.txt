In the 1988 presidential election, Carter ruled himself out as a candidate and predicted Vice President George H. W. Bush would be the Republican nominee.[63] Carter foresaw unity at the 1988 Democratic National Convention,[64] where he delivered an address.[65] After the election, which Bush won, Carter said Bush would have a more difficult presidency than Reagan because he was not as popular.[66]
During the 1992 presidential election, Carter met with Senator Paul Tsongas, who sought his advice.[67] Carter spoke favorably of former governor of Arkansas Bill Clinton,[68] and criticized Ross Perot, a Texas billionaire who was running as an independent.[69] As the primary concluded, Carter spoke of the need for the 1992 Democratic National Convention to address certain issues not focused on in the past,[70] and campaigned for Clinton after he became the Democratic nominee,[71] publicly stating his expectation to be consulted during Clinton's presidency.[72]
