Licensing of the Trump brand

The Trump name has been licensed for consumer products and services, including foodstuffs, apparel, learning courses, and home furnishings.[34][35] According to The Washington Post, there are more than 50 licensing or management deals involving Trump's name, and they have generated at least $59Â million in revenue for his companies.[36] By 2018, only two consumer goods companies continued to license his name.[34]
Side ventures
In September 1983, Trump purchased the New Jersey Generals, a team in the United States Football League. After the 1985 season, the league folded, largely due to Trump's attempt to move to a fall schedule (when it would have competed with the NFL for audience) and trying to force a merger with the NFL by bringing an antitrust suit.[37][38]
Trump and his Plaza Hotel hosted several boxing matches at the Atlantic City Convention Hall.[20][39] In 1989 and 1990, Trump lent his name to the Tour de Trump cycling stage race, an attempt to create an American equivalent of European races such as the Tour de France or the Giro d'Italia.[40]
