Emancipation Proclamation
Main articles: Abraham Lincoln and slavery and Emancipation Proclamation
The federal government's power to end slavery was limited by the Constitution, which before 1865, was understood to reserve the issue to the individual states. Lincoln believed that slavery would be rendered obsolete if its expansion into new territories were prevented, because these territories would be admitted to the Union as free states, and free states would come to outnumber slave states. He sought to persuade the states to agree to compensation for emancipating their slaves.[60] Lincoln rejected Major General John C.
