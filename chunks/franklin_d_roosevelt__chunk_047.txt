Germany annexed Austria in 1938, and soon turned its attention to its eastern neighbors.[77] Roosevelt made it clear that, in the event of German aggression against Czechoslovakia, the U.S. would remain neutral.[78] After completion of the Munich Agreement and the execution of Kristallnacht, American public opinion turned against Germany, and Roosevelt began preparing for a possible war with Germany.[79] Relying on an interventionist political coalition of Southern Democrats and business-oriented Republicans, Roosevelt oversaw the expansion of U.S. airpower and war production capacity.[80]
