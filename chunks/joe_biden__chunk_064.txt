Israel–Hamas war
Further information: United States support for Israel in the Israel–Hamas war
In October 2023, Hamas launched a surprise attack on Israel that devolved into an intensified conflict, jeopardizing the administration's push to normalize relations between Israel and Saudi Arabia.[225] Biden stated his unequivocal support for Israel and condemned the attack by Hamas.[226] He deployed aircraft carriers in the region to deter others from joining the war,[227] and called for an additional $14 billion in military aid to Israel.[228] He later began pressuring Israel to address the growing humanitarian crisis in the Gaza Strip.[229] Biden rejected calls for a ceasefire but said he supported "humanitarian pauses" to deliver aid to the people of the Gaza Strip.[230] He asked Israel to pause its invasion of Gaza for at least three days to allow for hostage negotiations; Israel agreed to daily four-hour pauses.[231] He also directed the U.S. military to facilitate the delivery of humanitarian aid to Palestinians in Gaza.[232] Biden has said he is a Zionist.[233][234] He has faced criticism for his unwavering support for Israel.
