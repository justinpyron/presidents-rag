Stewart and Harlan were conservative Republicans, while Brennan was a Democrat who became a leading voice for liberalism.[99] In selecting a Chief Justice, Eisenhower looked for an experienced jurist who could appeal to liberals in the party as well as law-and-order conservatives, noting privately that Warren "represents the kind of political, economic, and social thinking that I believe we need on the Supreme Court ... He has a national name for integrity, uprightness, and courage that, again, I believe we need on the Court".[100]
States admitted to the Union
Two states were admitted to the Union during Eisenhower's presidency.
Alaska – January 3, 1959 (49th state)
Hawaii – August 21, 1959 (50th state)
Health issues
Eisenhower began chain smoking cigarettes at West Point, often three or four packs a day. He joked that he "gave [himself] an order" to stop cold turkey in 1949.
