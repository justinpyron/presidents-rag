Afghanistan
In February 2021, having spoken to president Ashraf Ghani, Blinken voiced support for Afghan peace negotiations with Taliban Islamist rebels and reiterated the United States' commitment to a peace deal that includes a "just and durable political settlement and permanent and comprehensive ceasefire."[29]
Blinken made an unannounced visit to Kabul on April 15 and met with U.S. military and diplomatic personnel following the Biden administration's announcement of the 2021 withdrawal of U.S. troops from Afghanistan.[30] He said the decision to withdraw from Afghanistan was made to focus resources on China and the COVID-19 pandemic.[31] He faced calls to resign as secretary of state following the US withdrawal from Afghanistan.[32][33][34][35]
