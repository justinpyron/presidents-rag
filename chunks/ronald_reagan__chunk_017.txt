Main articles: Ronald Reagan 1976 presidential campaign and 1976 Republican Party presidential primaries

Insufficiently conservative to Reagan[1] and many other Republicans,[2] President Gerald Ford suffered from multiple political and economic woes. Ford, running for president, was disappointed to hear him also run.[3] Reagan was strongly critical of détente and Ford's policy of détente with the Soviet Union.[4] He repeated "A Time for Choosing" around the country[5] before announcing his campaign on November 20, 1975, when he discussed economic and social problems, and to a lesser extent, foreign affairs.[6] Both candidates were determined to knock each other out early in the primaries,[7] but Reagan would devastatingly lose the first five primaries beginning with New Hampshire,[8] where he popularized the welfare queen narrative about Linda Taylor, exaggerating her misuse of welfare benefits and igniting voter resentment for welfare reform,[9] but never overtly mentioning her name or race.[10]
