Death
At 12:25Â p.m. on March 28, 1969, Eisenhower died from congestive heart failure at Walter Reed Army Medical Center in Washington, D.C., at age 78.[8] The following day, his body was moved to the Washington National Cathedral's Bethlehem Chapel, where he lay in repose for 28 hours.[9] He was then transported to the United States Capitol, where he lay in state in the Capitol Rotunda on March 30 and 31.[10] A state funeral was conducted at the Washington National Cathedral on March 31.[11] The president and First Lady, Richard and Pat Nixon, attended, as did former president Lyndon Johnson. Also among the 2,000 guests that were invited were the UN Secretary-General U Thant and 191 foreign delegates from 78 countries, including 10 foreign heads of state and government. Guests included President Charles de Gaulle of France, who was in the United States for the first time since the state funeral of John F. Kennedy,[12] Chancellor Kurt-Georg Kiesinger of West Germany, King Baudouin of Belgium and Shah Mohammad Reza Pahlavi of Iran.[11]
The service included the singing of Faure's "The Palms", and the playing of the hymn "Onward, Christian Soldiers".[13]
