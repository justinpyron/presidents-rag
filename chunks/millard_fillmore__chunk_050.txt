He retained his position as Buffalo's leading citizen and was among those selected to escort the body when Lincoln's funeral train passed through Buffalo, but many remained angry at him for his wartime positions.[31] Fillmore supported President Andrew Johnson's Reconstruction policies since he felt that the nation needed to be reconciled as quickly as possible.[32] He devoted most of his time to civic activities. He aided Buffalo in becoming the third American city to have a permanent art gallery, the Buffalo Fine Arts Academy.[33]
Fillmore stayed in good health almost to the end of his life. He had a stroke in February 1874, and died on March 8, at age 74, after a second stroke. Two days later, he was buried at Forest Lawn Cemetery in Buffalo after a funeral procession including hundreds.[34] The U.S. Senate sent three of its members to honor Fillmore, including former vice president Hannibal Hamlin.[35]
