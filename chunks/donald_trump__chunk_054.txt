Initial response
The first confirmed case of COVID-19 in the U.S. was reported on January 20, 2020.[229] The outbreak was officially declared a public health emergency by Health and Human Services (HHS) Secretary Alex Azar on January 31, 2020.[230]
Trump initially ignored persistent public health warnings and calls for action from health officials within his administration and Secretary Azar.[231][232] Throughout January and February he focused on economic and political considerations of the outbreak.[233] In February 2020 Trump publicly asserted that the outbreak in the U.S. was less deadly than influenza, was "very much under control", and would soon be over.[234] On March 19, 2020, Trump privately told Bob Woodward that he was deliberately "playing it down, because I don't want to create a panic".[235][236]
