Adams's attempts to resolve this dispute failed, and he was often frustrated by a lack of news of progress from home.[38] The news he received of tumult at home, such as Shays' Rebellion, heightened his anxiety. Vice presidency (1789–1797)
Election
Main article: 1788–1789 United States presidential election
On June 17, 1788, Adams returned to a triumphant welcome in Massachusetts. He returned to farming life in the months after. The nation's first presidential election was soon to take place. Because George Washington was widely expected to win the presidency, many felt that the vice presidency should go to a northerner. Although he made no public comments on the matter, Adams was the primary contender.[1] Each state's presidential electors gathered on February 4, 1789, to cast their two votes for the president. The person with the most votes would be president and the second would become vice president.[2] Adams received 34 electoral college votes in the election, second behind Washington, who was a unanimous choice with 69 votes.
