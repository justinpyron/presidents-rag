George Washington was born on February 22, 1732,[a] at Popes Creek in Westmoreland County, Virginia.[2] He was the first of six children of Augustine and Mary Ball Washington.[3] His father was a justice of the peace and a prominent public figure who had four additional children from his first marriage to Jane Butler.[4] The family moved to Little Hunting Creek in 1734 before eventually settling in Ferry Farm near Fredericksburg, Virginia. When Augustine died in 1743, Washington inherited Ferry Farm and ten slaves; his older half-brother Lawrence inherited Little Hunting Creek and renamed it Mount Vernon.[5][6]
Washington did not have the formal education his elder half-brothers received at Appleby Grammar School in England, but he did attend the Lower Church School in Hartfield. He learned mathematics, including trigonometry, and land surveying, and became a talented draftsman and mapmaker.
