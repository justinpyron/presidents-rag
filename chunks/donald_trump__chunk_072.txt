Trump's lawyers did not deny the facts as presented in the charges, but said that Trump had not broken any laws or obstructed Congress.[360] They argued that the impeachment was "constitutionally and legally invalid" because Trump was not charged with a crime and that abuse of power is not an impeachable offense.[360]
On January 31, the Senate voted against allowing subpoenas for witnesses or documents.[361] The impeachment trial was the first in U.S. history without witness testimony.[362]
Trump was acquitted of both charges by the Republican majority. Senator Mitt Romney was the only Republican who voted to convict Trump on one charge, the abuse of power.[363] Following his acquittal, Trump fired impeachment witnesses and other political appointees and career officials he deemed insufficiently loyal.[364]
