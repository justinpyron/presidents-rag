The 2011 Egyptian protests posed the most challenging foreign policy crisis yet for the Obama administration.[35] Clinton's public response quickly evolved from an early assessment that the government of Hosni Mubarak was "stable", to a stance that there needed to be an "orderly transition [to] a democratic participatory government", to a condemnation of violence against the protesters.[36][37] Obama came to rely upon Clinton's advice, organization and personal connections in the behind-the-scenes response to developments.[35] As Arab Spring protests spread throughout the region, Clinton was at the forefront of a U.S. response that she recognized was sometimes contradictory, backing some regimes while supporting protesters against others.[38]
