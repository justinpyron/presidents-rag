Scholars and many Democrats initially viewed Carter's presidency as a failure.[15][16][17][18] In a 1982 Chicago Tribune survey, when 49 historians and scholars were asked to rank the best and worst U.S. presidents, Carter was ranked the 10th worst.[19] Since then, historical rankings of American presidents have generally ranked his presidency between 18th and 34th place.[20][21] The 2009 documentary Back Door Channels: The Price of Peace credits Carter's efforts at Camp David, which brought peace between Israel and Egypt, with bringing the only meaningful peace to the Middle East.[22][23] His post-presidency activities have been favorably received.
