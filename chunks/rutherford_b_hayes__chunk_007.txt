Lee's Army of Northern Virginia, which was advancing into Maryland.[9] Marching north, the 23rd was the lead regiment encountering the Confederates at the Battle of South Mountain on September 14.[10] Hayes led a charge against an entrenched position and was shot through his left arm, fracturing the bone.[11] He had one of his men tie a handkerchief above the wound in an effort to stop the bleeding, and continued to lead his men in the battle. While resting, he ordered his men to meet a flanking attack, but instead his entire command moved backward, leaving Hayes lying in between the lines.
