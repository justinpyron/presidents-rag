Personal life
In May 1991, The New York Times revealed that Bush had developed Graves' disease, a non-contagious thyroid condition that his wife Barbara also had.[1] Bush had two separate hip replacement surgeries in 2000 and 2007.[2] Thereafter, Bush started to experience weakness in his legs, which was attributed to vascular parkinsonism, a form of Parkinson's disease. He progressively developed problems walking, initially needing a walking stick for mobility aid before he eventually came to rely on a wheelchair from 2011 onwards.[3]
Bush was a lifelong Episcopalian and a member of St. Martin's Episcopal Church in Houston.
