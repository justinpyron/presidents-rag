Washington frequently suffered from severe tooth decay and ultimately lost all his teeth but one. He had several sets of false teeth during his presidency. Contrary to common lore, these were not made of wood, but of metal, ivory, bone, animal teeth, and human teeth possibly obtained from slaves.[9][10][11] These dental problems left him in constant pain, which he treated with laudanum.[12]
Washington was a talented equestrian, with Thomas Jefferson describing him as "the best horseman of his age".[13] He collected thoroughbreds at Mount Vernon, his two favorite horses being Blueskin and Nelson.[14] He enjoyed hunting foxes, deer, ducks, and other game.[15] He was an excellent dancer and frequently attended the theater. He drank alcohol in moderation but was morally opposed to excessive drinking, smoking tobacco, gambling, and profanity.[16]
