Election to Congress
Main article: 1789 Virginia's 5th congressional district election
After Virginia ratified the constitution, Madison returned to New York and resumed his duties in the Congress of the Confederation. After Madison was defeated in his bid for the Senate, and with concerns for both his political career and the possibility that Patrick Henry and his allies would arrange for a second constitutional convention, Madison ran for the House of Representatives.[1][2][3] Henry and the Anti-Federalists were in firm control of the General Assembly in the autumn of 1788.[3] At Henry's behest, the Virginia legislature designed to deny Madison a seat by gerrymandering congressional districts.
