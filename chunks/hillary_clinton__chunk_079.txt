LGBT rights
Clinton supports the right to same-sex marriage, a position that has developed throughout her political career.[11] In 2000, she was against such marriages altogether. In 2006, she said only that she would support a state's decision to permit same-sex marriages, but opposed federally amending the Constitution to permit same-sex marriage. While running for president in 2007, she again reiterated her opposition to same-sex marriage, although she expressed her support of civil unions.[16][17] 2013 marked the first time that Clinton expressed support for a national right to same-sex marriage.[17] In 2016, she was the first major-party presidential candidate ever to write an op-ed for an LGBT newspaper, the Philadelphia Gay News.[18]
