The Carter Center announced Jimmy Carter 100: A Celebration in Song, an event concert to celebrate Carter's 100th birthday that featured appearances by musicians and celebrities. The event took place on September 17, 2024, at the Fox Theatre in Atlanta, Georgia.[26] On October 1, 2024, Carter turned 100, the first president to do so.[27] Local events to celebrate his birthday included a F-18 Super Hornet flyover formation by eight Navy pilots from Naval Air Station Oceana, which Carter viewed from his backyard, and a naturalization ceremony for 100 new citizens at Plains High School, which Chip Carter attended.[28][29]
Carter has made arrangements to be buried in front of his home at 209 Woodland Drive in Plains. In 2006 he said that a funeral in Washington, D.C., with visitation at the Carter Center was also planned.[30] Carter asked President Biden to deliver his eulogy.[31]
