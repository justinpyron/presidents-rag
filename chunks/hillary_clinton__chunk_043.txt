Following the final primaries on June 3, 2008, Obama had gained enough delegates to become the presumptive nominee.[28] In a speech before her supporters on June 7, Clinton ended her campaign and endorsed Obama.[29] By campaign's end, Clinton had won 1,640 pledged delegates to Obama's 1,763;[30] at the time of the clinching, Clinton had 286 superdelegates to Obama's 395,[31] with those numbers widening to 256 versus 438 once Obama was acknowledged the winner.[30] Clinton and Obama each received over 17 million votes during the nomination process[b] with both breaking the previous record.[32] Clinton was the first woman to run in the primary or caucus of every state and she eclipsed, by a very wide margin, Congresswoman Shirley Chisholm's 1972 marks for most votes garnered and delegates won by a woman.[13] Clinton gave a passionate speech supporting Obama at the 2008 Democratic National Convention and campaigned frequently for him in fall 2008, which concluded with his victory over McCain in the general election on November 4.[33]
