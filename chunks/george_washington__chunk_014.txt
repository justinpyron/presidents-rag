On August 1, Washington attended the First Virginia Convention. There, he was selected as a delegate to the First Continental Congress.[33] As tensions rose in 1774, he helped train militias in Virginia and organized enforcement of the Continental Association boycott of British goods instituted by the Congress.[34]
The American Revolutionary War broke out on April 19, 1775, with the Battles of Lexington and Concord and the Siege of Boston.[35] Upon hearing the news, Washington was "sobered and dismayed",[36] and he hastily departed Mount Vernon on May 4, 1775, to join the Second Continental Congress in Philadelphia.[37]
