His speech was well received and elevated his status within the Democratic Party.[25]
Obama's expected opponent in the general election, Republican primary winner Jack Ryan, withdrew from the race in June 2004.[26] Six weeks later, Alan Keyes accepted the Republican nomination to replace Ryan.[27] In the November 2004 general election, Obama won with 70 percent of the vote, the largest margin of victory for a Senate candidate in Illinois history.[28] He took 92 of the state's 102 counties, including several where Democrats traditionally do not do well.
