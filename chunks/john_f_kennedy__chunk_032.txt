Peace Corps
Main article: Peace Corps
In one of his first presidential acts, Kennedy signed Executive Order 10924 that officially started the Peace Corps. He named his brother-in-law, Sargent Shriver, as its first director.[19] Through this program, Americans volunteered to help developing countries in fields like education, farming, health care, and construction.[20] Kennedy believed that countries that received Peace Corps volunteers were less likely to succumb to a communist revolution.[21] Tanganyika (present-day Tanzania) and Ghana were the first countries to participate.[22] The organization grew to 5,000 members by March 1963 and 10,000 the year after.[23] Since 1961, over 200,000 Americans have joined the Peace Corps, representing 139 countries.[24][25]
