In June 2020, during the George Floyd protests, federal law-enforcement officials controversially used less lethal weapons to remove a largely peaceful crowd of lawful protesters from Lafayette Square, outside the White House.[61][62] Trump then posed with a Bible for a photo-op at the nearby St. John's Episcopal Church,[61][63][64] with religious leaders condemning both the treatment of protesters and the photo opportunity itself.[65] Many retired military leaders and defense officials condemned Trump's proposal to use the U.S. military against anti-police-brutality protesters.[66]
