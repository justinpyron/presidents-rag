Cultural and political image
Main article: Public image of George W. Bush
See also: Efforts to impeach George W. Bush
Image
Bush's upbringing in West Texas, his accent, his vacations to his Texas ranch, and his penchant for country metaphors contribute to his folksy, American cowboy image.[246][247] "I think people look at him and think John Wayne", said Piers Morgan, editor of the British Daily Mirror.[248]
Bush has been parodied by the media,[249] comedians, and other politicians.[250] Detractors tended to cite linguistic errors made by Bush during his public speeches, which are colloquially referred to as Bushisms.[251]
In contrast to his father, who was perceived as having troubles with an overarching unifying theme, Bush embraced larger visions and was seen as a man of larger ideas and associated huge risks.[252]
Tony Blair wrote in 2010 that the caricature of Bush as being dumb is "ludicrous" and that Bush is "very smart".[253] In an interview with Playboy, The New York Times columnist David Brooks said Bush "was 60 IQ points smarter in private than he was in public. He doesn't want anybody to think he's smarter than they are, so he puts on a Texas act."[254][unreliable source?]
