He conducted the district's affairs for five weeks until the Louisiana Territory was formally established on July 4, 1805, and Brigadier General James Wilkinson assumed the duties of governor.[17]
In 1805, Harrison built a plantation-style home near Vincennes that he named Grouseland, in tribute to the birds on the property.[18] The 26-room home was one of the first brick structures in the territory;[19] and it served as a center of social and political life in the territory during his tenure as governor.[20] Harrison founded a university at Vincennes in 1801, which was incorporated as Vincennes University on November 29, 1806.[21] The territorial capital was eventually moved to Corydon in 1813, and Harrison built a second home at nearby Harrison Valley.[22]
Harrison's primary responsibility was to obtain title to Indian lands that would allow future settlement and increase the territory's population, a requirement for statehood.
