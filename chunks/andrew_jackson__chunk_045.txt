Panic of 1837
Main article: Panic of 1837
Despite the economic boom following Jackson's victory in the Bank War, land speculation in the west caused the Panic of 1837.[119]  Jackson's transfer of federal monies to state banks in 1833 caused western banks to relax their lending standards;[120] the Indian Removal Act made large amounts of former Native American lands available for purchase and speculation.[121] Two of Jackson's acts in 1836 contributed to the Panic of 1837. One was the Specie Circular, which mandated western lands only be purchased by money backed by specie.
