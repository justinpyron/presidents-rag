The idea was never commercialized, but it made Lincoln the only president to hold a patent.[5]
Lincoln appeared before the Illinois Supreme Court in 175 cases; he was sole counsel in 51 cases, of which 31 were decided in his favor.[6] From 1853 to 1860, one of his largest clients was the Illinois Central Railroad.[7] His legal reputation gave rise to the nickname "Honest Abe".[8]
In an 1858 criminal trial, Lincoln represented William "Duff" Armstrong, who was on trial for the murder of James Preston Metzker.[9] The case is famous for Lincoln's use of a fact established by judicial notice to challenge the credibility of an eyewitness. After an opposing witness testified to seeing the crime in the moonlight, Lincoln produced a Farmers' Almanac showing the Moon was at a low angle, drastically reducing visibility. Armstrong was acquitted.[9]
