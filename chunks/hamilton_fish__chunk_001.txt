Jay, served as president of the Philolexian Society, and was admitted to the New York bar in 1830, practicing briefly with William Beach Lawrence.[3][4] Influenced politically by his father, Fish aligned himself to the Whig Party.[3] He served as commissioner of deeds for the city and county of New York from 1831[5] through 1833, and was an unsuccessful Whig candidate for New York State Assembly in 1834.[4]
On December 15, 1836, Fish married Julia Kean, a sister of Col. John Kean, both descendants of William Livingston, a New Yorker who went on to become New Jersey's first governor.[6] The couple's lengthy married life was described as happy and Mrs. Fish was known for her "sagacity and judgement."[6] The couple had three sons and five daughters.[6]
