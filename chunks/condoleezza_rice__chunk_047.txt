In August 2005, American musician, actor, and social activist Harry Belafonte, who served on the Board of TransAfrica, referred to blacks in the Bush administration as "black tyrants." Belafonte's comments received mixed reactions.[15]
Rice dismissed these criticisms during a September 14, 2005 interview when she said, "Why would I worry about something like that? ... The fact of the matter is I've been black all my life. Nobody needs to tell me how to be black."[17]
Black commentators have defended Rice, including Mike Espy,[18] Andrew Young,[19] C. Delores Tucker (chair of the National Congress of Black Women),[19] Clarence Page,[20] Colbert King,[21] Dorothy Height (chair and president emerita of the National Council of Negro Women)[21] and Kweisi Mfume (Congressman and former CEO of the NAACP).[22]
Family and personal life
Rice has never married and has no children.[1] In the 1970s, she dated and was briefly engaged to professional American football player Rick Upchurch but left him because, according to biographer Marcus Mabry, she "knew the relationship wasn't going to work."[2]
Rice's mother, Angelena Rice, died of breast cancer in 1985, aged 61, when Rice was 30.[3] In 1989, Rice's father, John Wesley Rice, wed Clara Bailey,[4] to whom he remained married until his death in  2000, aged 77.[5][self-published source?]
