However, Gorbachev added the condition that SDI research must be confined to laboratories during the ten-year period when disarmament would take place. Reagan refused, stating that it was defensive only and that he would share the secrets with the Soviets, thus failing to reach a deal.[166]
In June 1987, Reagan addressed Gorbachev during a speech at the Berlin Wall, demanding that he "tear down this wall". The remark was ignored at the time, but after the wall fell in November 1989, it was retroactively recast as a soaring achievement.[167][168][169] In December, Reagan and Gorbachev met again at the Washington Summit[170] to sign the Intermediate-Range Nuclear Forces Treaty, committing to the total abolition of their respective short-range and medium-range missile stockpiles.[171] The treaty established an inspections regime designed to ensure that both parties honored the agreement.[172] In May 1988, the U.S. Senate overwhelmingly voted in favor of ratifying the treaty,[173] providing a major boost to Reagan's popularity in the aftermath of the Iran–Contra affair.
