Jefferson consulted with his fellow committee members, but mostly wrote the Declaration of Independence in isolation between June 11 and 28, 1776, in a home he was renting at 700 Market Street in Center City Philadelphia.[1] Jefferson drew considerably on his proposed draft of the Virginia Constitution, George Mason's draft of the Virginia Declaration of Rights, and other sources.[7] Other committee members made some changes, and a final draft was presented to Congress on June 28, 1776.[8]
The declaration was introduced on Friday, June 28, and Congress began debate over its contents on Monday, July 1,[8] resulting in the removal of roughly a fourth of Jefferson's original draft.[9][10] Jefferson resented the changes, but he did not speak publicly about the revisions.[b] On July 4, 1776, the Congress ratified the Declaration, and delegates signed it on August 2; in so doing, the delegates were knowingly committing an act of high treason against The Crown, which was deemed the most serious criminal offense and was punishable by torture and death.[12]
