She began work on another volume of memoirs and made appearances on the paid speaking circuit.[9] There she received $200,000–225,000 per engagement, often appearing before Wall Street firms or at business conventions.[9][10] She also made some unpaid speeches on behalf of the foundation.[9] For the fifteen months ending in March 2015, Clinton earned over $11 million from her speeches.[11] For the overall period 2007–14, the Clintons earned almost $141 million, paid some $56 million in federal and state taxes and donated about $15 million to charity.[12] As of 2015[update], she was estimated to be worth over $30 million on her own, or $45–53 million with her husband.[13]
Clinton resigned from the board of the Clinton Foundation in April 2015, when she began her presidential campaign. The foundation said it would accept new foreign governmental donations from six Western nations only.[a]
