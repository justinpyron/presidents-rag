Budget surplus and civil service reform
The Delaware legislature re-elected Bayard to the Senate for a third term in 1881 without serious opposition.[67] The Senate in the 47th Congress was evenly divided between Republicans and Democrats, with the new vice president, Arthur, holding the tie-breaking vote.[d] After spending the special session of March 1881 in an intra-Republican Party fight over the confirmation of Garfield's cabinet nominees, the Senate went into recess until October.[68] By that time, Garfield had been assassinated and Arthur was president.[68] When the Senate reconvened, the Democrats held the majority briefly, and Bayard was elected president pro tempore on October 10; Republicans regained the majority three days later as Republican latecomers arrived and were sworn in, and David Davis took over the office.[68]
