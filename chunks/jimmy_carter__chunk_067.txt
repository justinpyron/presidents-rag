Other activities
In 1982, Carter founded the Carter Center,[105] a non-governmental and non-profit organization with the purpose of advancing human rights and alleviating human suffering,[106] including helping improve the quality of life for people in more than 80 countries.[107] Among these efforts has been the contribution of the Carter Center working alongside the World Health Organization to the near-eradication of dracunculiasis, also called Guinea worm disease. The incidence of this disease has decreased from 3.5 million cases in the mid-1980s, to 25 cases in 2016,[108][109] and four in the first seven months of 2024, according to the Carter Center's statistics.[110]
Carter attended the dedication of his presidential library[111] and those of Presidents Ronald Reagan,[112] George H. W. Bush,[113] Bill Clinton,[114][115] and George W. Bush.[116] He delivered eulogies at the funerals of Coretta Scott King,[117] Gerald Ford,[118][119] and Theodore Hesburgh.[120]
In 2007, Carter founded the New Baptist Covenant organization for social justice.[121][122]
