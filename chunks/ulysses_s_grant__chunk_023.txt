On April 16, Grant ordered Admiral David Dixon Porter's gunboats south under fire from the Vicksburg batteries to meet up with troops who had marched south down the west side of the river.[75] Grant ordered diversionary battles, confusing Pemberton and allowing Grant's army to move east across the Mississippi.[76] Grant's army captured Jackson. Advancing west, he defeated Pemberton's army at the Battle of Champion Hill on May 16, forcing their retreat into Vicksburg.[77]
After Grant's men assaulted the entrenchments twice, suffering severe losses, they settled in for a siege which lasted seven weeks. During quiet periods of the campaign, Grant would drink on occasion.[78] The personal rivalry between McClernand and Grant continued until Grant removed him from command when he contravened Grant by publishing an order without permission.[79] Pemberton surrendered Vicksburg to Grant on July 4, 1863.[80]
Vicksburg's fall gave Union forces control of the Mississippi River and split the Confederacy.
