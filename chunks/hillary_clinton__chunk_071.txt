This was in line with the average bounce in conventions since 2004, although it was toward the low side by historical standards.[28][29][30] Following Clinton's seven percentage point convention bounce at the Democratic National Convention, she regained a significant lead in national polls at the start of August.[31][32] In fall 2016, Clinton and Tim Kaine published Stronger Together, which outlined their vision for the United States.[33]
Clinton was defeated by Donald Trump in the November 8, 2016, presidential election.[34] By the early morning hours of November 9, Trump had received 279 projected electoral college votes, with 270 needed to win; media sources proclaimed him the winner.[35] Clinton then phoned Trump to concede and to congratulate him on his victory, whereupon Trump gave his victory speech.[36] The next morning Clinton made a public concession speech in which she acknowledged the pain of her loss, but called on her supporters to accept Trump as their next president, saying: "We owe him an open mind and a chance to lead."[37] Though Clinton lost the election by capturing only 232 electoral votes to Trump's 306, she won the popular vote by more than 2.8Â million votes, or 2.1% of the voter base.[38][39] She is the fifth presidential candidate
