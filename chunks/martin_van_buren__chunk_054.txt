Neither Buchanan nor Pierce was willing to make Pierce's proposal public, and nothing more resulted from it.[47] Once the American Civil War began, Van Buren made public his support for the Union cause.[48]
Death
Van Buren's health began to fail later in 1861, and he was bedridden with pneumonia during the fall and winter of 1861–1862.[49] He died of bronchial asthma and heart failure at his Lindenwald estate at 2:00 a.m. on Thursday, July 24, 1862.[50] He is buried in the Kinderhook Reformed Dutch Church Cemetery, as are his wife Hannah, his parents, and his son Martin Van Buren Jr.[51]
Van Buren outlived all four of his immediate successors: Harrison, Tyler, Polk, and Taylor.[52] In addition, he saw more successors ascend to the presidency than anyone else (eight), living to see Abraham Lincoln elected as the 16th President before his death.[53]
