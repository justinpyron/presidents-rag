He also managed some real estate he had acquired in Duluth, Minnesota.[20] Not entirely removed from politics, Hayes held out some hope of a cabinet appointment, but was disappointed to receive only an appointment as assistant U.S. treasurer at Cincinnati, which he turned down.[21] He agreed to be nominated for his old House seat in 1872 but was disappointed when he lost the election to Henry B. Banning, a fellow Kenyon College alumnus.[22]
In 1873, Lucy gave birth to another son, Manning Force Hayes.[23][b] That same year, the Panic of 1873 hurt business prospects across the nation, including Hayes's. His uncle Sardis Birchard died that year, and the Hayes family moved into Spiegel Grove, the grand house Birchard had built with them in mind.[25] That year Hayes announced his uncle's bequest of $50,000 in assets to endow a public library for Fremont, to be called the Birchard Library. It opened in 1874 on Front Street, and a new building was completed and opened in 1878 in Fort Stephenson State Park, as per the terms of the bequest. Hayes served as chairman of the library's board of trustees until his death.[26]
