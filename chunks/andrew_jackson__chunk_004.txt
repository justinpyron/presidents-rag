While boarding at the home of Rachel Stockly Donelson, the widow of John Donelson, Jackson became acquainted with their daughter, Rachel Donelson Robards. The younger Rachel was in an unhappy marriage with Captain Lewis Robards, and the two were separated by 1789.[17] After the separation, Jackson and Rachel became romantically involved,[18] living together as husband and wife.[19] Robards petitioned for divorce, which was granted in 1793 on the basis of Rachel's infidelity.[20] The couple legally married in January 1794.[21] In 1796, they acquired their first plantation, Hunter's Hill,[22] on 640 acres (260Â ha) of land near Nashville.[23]
