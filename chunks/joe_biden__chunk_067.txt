NATO enlargement
Following the 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine, Biden expressed support for expanding NATO to cover Sweden and Finland.[254][255] On August 9, 2022, he signed the instruments of ratification stipulating U.S. support for the two countries' entry into NATO.[256][257] Finnish ascension occurred on April 4, 2023, but opposition by Turkey and Hungary to Swedish entry led to a stalemate.[258] Biden led diplomatic talks resulting in formal Swedish ascension into NATO on March 7, 2024.[259][260] He has also expressed openness to Ukrainian entry into NATO following the end of the conflict,[261] supporting an expedited timetable in its ascension and the removal of steps such as the Membership Action Plan typically required for NATO entry.[262][263]
