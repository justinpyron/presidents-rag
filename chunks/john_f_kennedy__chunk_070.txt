Webb to head NASA.[210]
In Kennedy's State of the Union address in 1961, he suggested international cooperation in space. Khrushchev declined, as the Soviets did not wish to reveal the status of their rocketry and space capabilities.[211] In April 1961, Soviet cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin became the first person to fly in space, reinforcing American fears about being left behind by the Soviet Union.[212] Less than a month later, Alan Shepard became the first American to travel into space, strengthening Kennedy's confidence in NASA.[213] The following year, John Glenn, aboard the Mercury craft Friendship 7, became the first American to orbit the Earth.[214]
