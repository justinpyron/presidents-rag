Though he praised President Barack Obama in the early part of his tenure,[47] Carter stated his disagreement with using drone strikes against suspected terrorists, Obama's choice to keep Guantanamo Bay detention camp open,[48] and the federal surveillance programs Edward Snowden revealed.[49][50]
During Donald Trump's presidency, Carter spoke favorably of the chance for immigration reform[51] and criticized Trump for his handling of the U.S. national anthem protests.[52] In October 2017, he defended Trump in an interview with The New York Times, criticizing the media's coverage of him as harsher "than any other president certainly that I've known about".[53][54] In 2019, Trump called Carter and expressed concern that China was "getting ahead" of the United States. Carter agreed, saying that China's strength came from its lack of involvement in armed conflict and calling the U.S. "the most warlike nation in the history of the world."[55]
