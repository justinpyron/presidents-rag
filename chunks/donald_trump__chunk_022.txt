Trump withdrew from public activities in the weeks following the election.[89] He initially blocked government officials from cooperating in Biden's presidential transition.[90][91] After three weeks, the administrator of the General Services Administration declared Biden the "apparent winner" of the election, allowing the disbursement of transition resources to his team.[92] Trump still did not formally concede while claiming he recommended the GSA begin transition protocols.[93][94]
The Electoral College formalized Biden's victory on December 14.[81] From November to January, Trump repeatedly sought help to overturn the results, personally pressuring Republican local and state office-holders,[95] Republican state and federal legislators,[96] the Justice Department,[97] and Vice President Pence,[98] urging various actions such as replacing presidential electors, or a request for Georgia officials to "find" votes and announce a "recalculated" result.[96] On February 10, 2021, Georgia prosecutors opened a criminal investigation into Trump's efforts to subvert the election in Georgia.[99]
Trump did not attend Biden's inauguration.[100]
