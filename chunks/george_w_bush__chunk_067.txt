Publications and appearances
Since leaving office, Bush has kept a relatively low profile.[9] Bush has spoken in favor of increased global participation of women in politics and societal matters in foreign countries.[10][11]
In March 2009, he delivered his first post-presidency speech in Calgary, Alberta,[12][13] appeared via video on The Colbert Report during which he praised U.S. troops for earning a "special place in American history",[14] and attended the funeral of Senator Ted Kennedy.[15] Bush made his debut as a motivational speaker on October 26 at the "Get Motivated" seminar in Dallas.[16] In the aftermath of the Fort Hood shooting on November 5, 2009, the Bushes paid an undisclosed visit to the survivors and the victims' families the day following the shooting, having contacted the base commander requesting that the visit be private and not involve press coverage.[17]
