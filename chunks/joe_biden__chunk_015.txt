Biden became interested in the Yugoslav Wars after hearing about Serbian abuses during the Croatian War of Independence in 1991.[12] Once the Bosnian War broke out, Biden was among the first to call for the "lift and strike" policy.[12][51] The George H. W. Bush administration and Clinton administration were both reluctant to implement the policy, fearing Balkan entanglement.[12][52] In April 1993, Biden held a tense three-hour meeting with Serbian leader Slobodan Milošević.[55] Biden worked on several versions of legislative language urging the U.S. toward greater involvement.[55] Biden has called his role in affecting Balkan policy in the mid-1990s his "proudest moment in public life" related to foreign policy.[52] In 1999, during the Kosovo War, Biden supported the 1999 NATO bombing of FR Yugoslavia.[12] He and Senator John McCain co-sponsored the McCain-Biden Kosovo Resolution, which called on Clinton to use all necessary force, including ground troops, to confront Milošević over Yugoslav actions toward ethnic Albanians in Kosovo.[52][56]
