In 2011, Obama signed a four-year renewal of the Patriot Act.[19] Following the 2013 global surveillance disclosures by whistleblower Edward Snowden, Obama condemned the leak as unpatriotic,[20] but called for increased restrictions on the National Security Agency (NSA) to address violations of privacy.[21][22] Obama continued and expanded surveillance programs set up by George W. Bush, while implementing some reforms.[23] He supported legislation that would have limited the NSA's ability to collect phone records in bulk under a single program and supported bringing more transparency to the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court (FISC).[23]
