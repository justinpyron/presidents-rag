Rice's mother, Angelena Rice, died of breast cancer in 1985, aged 61, when Rice was 30.[3] In 1989, Rice's father, John Wesley Rice, wed Clara Bailey,[4] to whom he remained married until his death in  2000, aged 77.[5][self-published source?]
From 2003 to 2017, Rice co-owned a house in Palo Alto, California with Randy Bean. According to public records, the two initially bought the house with a third investor, Stanford University professor Coit D. Blacker, who later sold his line of credit to the two women. The property arrangement was first revealed in Glenn Kessler's book The Confidante: Condoleezza Rice and the Creation of the Bush Legacy (2007), sparking rumors about the nature of Rice and Bean's relationship. Kessler has stated he "did not know if this meant there was something more to the relationship between the women beyond a friendship."[6][7][8][9]
On August 20, 2012, Rice was one of the first two women to be admitted as members to Augusta National Golf Club; the other was South Carolina financier Darla Moore.[10] In 2014, Rice was named to the ESPNW Impact 25.[11] Rice has described her faith as Presbyterian, being a noted member of ECO: A Covenant Order of Evangelical Presbyterians.[12][13][14][15][16]
