It was Reagan's belief that communism was a powerful backstage influence in Hollywood that led him to rally his friends against them.[2]
Reagan began shifting to the right when he supported the presidential campaigns of Dwight D. Eisenhower in 1952 and Richard Nixon in 1960.[7] When Reagan was contracted by General Electric (GE), he gave speeches to their employees. His speeches had a positive take on free markets.[8] Under GE vice president Lemuel Boulware, a staunch anti-communist,[9] employees were encouraged to vote for business-friendly politicians.[10]
In 1961, Reagan adapted his speeches into another speech to criticize Medicare.[11] In his view, its legislation would have meant "the end of individual freedom in the United States".[12] In 1962, Reagan was dropped by GE,[13] and he formally registered as a Republican.[7]
In the 1964 U.S. presidential election, Reagan gave a speech for presidential contender Barry Goldwater[14] that was eventually referred to as "A Time for Choosing".[15] Reagan argued that the Founding Fathers "knew that governments don't control things.
