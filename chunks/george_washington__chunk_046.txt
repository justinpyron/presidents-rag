Washington planned to resign after his first term, but political strife convinced him to remain in office.[10] He was an able administrator and a judge of talent and character, and he regularly talked with department heads to get their advice.[11] He tolerated opposing views, despite fears that a democratic system would lead to political violence, and he conducted a smooth transition of power to his successor.[12] He remained non-partisan throughout his presidency (the only United States president to do so), and opposed the divisiveness of political parties, but he favored a strong central government, was sympathetic to a Federalist form of government, and leery of the Republican opposition.[13]
