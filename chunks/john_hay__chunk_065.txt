In 1923 Mount Hay, also known as Boundary Peak 167 on the Canada–United States border, was named after John Hay in recognition of his role in negotiating the US-Canada treaty resulting in the Alaska Boundary Tribunal.[12] Brown University's John Hay Library is named for him as well.[13] Hay's New Hampshire estate has been conserved by various organizations.[14][15] Although he and his family never lived there (Hay died while it was under construction), the Hay-McKinney House, home to the Cleveland History Center and thousands of artifacts, serves to remind Clevelanders of John Hay's lengthy service.[16] During World War II the Liberty ship SS John Hay was built in Panama City, Florida, and named in his honor.[17] Camp John Hay a United States military base established in 1903 in Baguio, Philippines, was named for John Hay, and the base name was maintained by the Philippine government even after its 1991 turnover to Philippine authorities.[18]
