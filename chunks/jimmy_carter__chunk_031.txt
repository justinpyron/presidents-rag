Economy
Carter's presidency had a troubled economic history of two roughly equal periods. The first two years were a time of intense stagflation, primarily due to recovery from the severe 1973–75 recession, which had left fixed investment at its lowest level since the 1970 recession and unemployment at 9%.[44] Under Carter, the unemployment rate declined from 8.1% when he took office to 5.7% by July 1978,[45][46] but the early 1980s recession caused it to return to its pre-1977 level.[47] The Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) measured a 6.6% unemployment rate average during the Carter administration.[48] His last two years were marked by double-digit inflation, very high interest rates,[49] oil shortages, and slow economic growth.[50] Due to the $30 billion economic stimu
