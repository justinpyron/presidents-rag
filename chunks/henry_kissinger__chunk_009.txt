By this time, he was arguably "one of the most important theorists about foreign policy ever to be produced by the United States", according to his official biographer Niall Ferguson.[20]
Foreign policy
Kissinger served as National Security Advisor and Secretary of State under President Richard Nixon and continued as Secretary of State under Nixon's successor Gerald Ford.[1] With the death of George Shultz in February 2021, Kissinger was the last surviving member of the Nixon administration Cabinet.[2]
