Carter and Mondale were formally nominated at the 1980 Democratic National Convention in New York City.[162] Carter delivered a speech notable for its tribute to the late Hubert Humphrey, whom he initially called "Hubert Horatio Hornblower",[163] and Kennedy made "The Dream Shall Never Die" speech, in which he criticized Reagan and did not endorse Carter.[164]
Along with Reagan and Kennedy, Carter was opposed by centrist John B. Anderson, who had previously contested the Republican presidential primaries, and upon losing to Reagan, reentered the race as an independent. Anderson advertised himself as a more liberal alternative to Reagan's conservatism.[165] As the campaign went on, Anderson's polling numbers dropped and his base was gradually pulled to Carter or Reagan.[166] Carter had to run against his own "stagflation"-ridden economy, while the hostage crisis in Iran dominated the news every week.
