Officials have urged him to take a harder stance against Israel, criticizing his administration's leniency and support despite the Israeli government's contentious offensive, which has led to significant civilian casualties and humanitarian crises.[235][236][237]
Following the killing of Palestinian civilians receiving food aid on February 29, 2024, Biden said the current level of aid flowing into Gaza was insufficient.[238] On March 3, the U.S. military began airdropping food aid into Gaza.[239] Several experts called the U.S. airdrops performative and said they would do little to alleviate the famine in Gaza.[240]
