His original interment was temporary, in the public vault at the Congressional Cemetery in Washington, D.C. Later, he was interred in the family burial ground in Quincy, Massachusetts, across from the (Unitarian) United First Parish Church, called Hancock Cemetery. After Louisa's death in 1852, his son had his parents re-interred in the expanded family crypt in the United First Parish Church across the street, next to John and Abigail. Both tombs are viewable by the public. Adams's original tomb at Hancock Cemetery is still there and marked simply "J.Q. Adams".[54]
Personal life
See also: Adams political family
Adams and Louisa had three sons and a daughter. Their daughter, Louisa, was born in 1811, but died in 1812,[1] from unknown reasons. They named their first son George Washington Adams (1801–1829) after the first president.
