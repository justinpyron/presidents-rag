The SAG fought with film producers for the right to receive residual payments,[34] and on November 16, 1959, the board elected Reagan SAG president for the second time;[35] he replaced Howard Keel, who had resigned. During this second stint, Reagan managed to secure payments for actors whose theatrical films had been released between 1948 and 1959 and subsequently televised. The producers were initially required to pay the actors fees, but they ultimately settled instead for providing pensions and paying residuals for films made after 1959. Reagan resigned from the SAG presidency on June 7, 1960, and also left the board;[36] George Chandler succeeded him as SAG president.[37]
Marriages and children
In January 1940, Reagan married Jane Wyman, his co-star in the 1938 film Brother Rat.[38][39] Together, they had two biological daughters: Maureen in 1941,[40] and Christine in 1947 (born prematurely and died the following day).[41] They adopted one son, Michael, in 1945.[21] Wyman filed to divorce Reagan in June 1948.
