Historian David Herbert Donald described the speech as "a superb political move for an unannounced presidential aspirant. Appearing in Seward's home state, sponsored by a group largely loyal to Chase, Lincoln shrewdly made no reference to either of these Republican rivals for the nomination."[43] In response to an inquiry about his ambitions, Lincoln said, "The taste is in my mouth a little".[44]
