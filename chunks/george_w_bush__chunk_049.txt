On October 17, 2006, Bush signed the Military Commissions Act of 2006 into law.[211] The new rule was enacted in the wake of the Supreme Court's decision in Hamdan v. Rumsfeld, 548 U.S. 557 (2006),[212] which allowed the U.S. government to prosecute unlawful enemy combatants by military commission rather than a standard trial.
