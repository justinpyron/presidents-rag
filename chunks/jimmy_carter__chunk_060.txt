In July 2001, Carter said he was "disappointed in almost everything" President George W. Bush had done, but after the September 11 attacks, he offered only praise, calling on Americans to support Bush with "complete unity".[39] Later, Carter opposed the Iraq War[40] and what he considered an attempt by Bush and Tony Blair to oust Saddam Hussein with "lies and misinterpretations".[41] In 2004, Carter said that he believed Bush exploited the September 11 attacks and blamed the media for not criticizing him.[42] In 2007, Carter said the Bush administration "has been the worst in history" on foreign affairs;[43] he later said he was just comparing Bush's tenure to Nixon's.[44] Tony Fratto responded to Carter's comments on the Bush administration's behalf by saying that the comments increased Carter's irrelevance.[45] By the end of Bush's second term, Carter considered Bush's tenure disappointing, as he told Forward Magazine of Syria.[46]
Though he praised President Barack Obama in the early part of his tenure,[47] Carter stated his disagreement with using drone strikes against suspected terrorists, Obama's choice to keep Guantanamo Bay detention camp open,[48] and the federal surveillance programs Edward Snowden revealed.[49][50]
