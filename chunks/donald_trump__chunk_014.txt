2016 presidential campaign
Main article: Donald Trump 2016 presidential campaign
Further information: 2016 Republican Party presidential primaries and 2016 United States presidential election § General election campaign
Trump's fame and provocative statements earned him an unprecedented amount of free media coverage, elevating his standing in the Republican primaries.[12] He adopted the phrase "truthful hyperbole", coined by his ghostwriter Tony Schwartz, to describe his public speaking style.[13][14] His campaign statements were often opaque and suggestive,[15] and a record number were false.[16][17][18] Trump said he disdained political correctness and frequently made claims of media bias.[19][20]
Trump announced his candidacy in June 2015.[21][22] His campaign was initially not taken seriously by political analysts, but he quickly rose to the top of opinion polls.[23] He became the front-runner in March 2016[24] and was declared the presumptive Republican nominee in May.[25]
