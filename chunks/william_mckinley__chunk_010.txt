As McKinley's professional career progressed, so too did his social life blossom: he wooed Ida Saxton, a daughter of a prominent Canton family.[5] They were married on January 25, 1871, in the newly built First Presbyterian Church of Canton. Ida soon joined her husband's Methodist church.[6] Their first child, Katherine, was born on Christmas Day 1871.[6] A second daughter, Ida, followed in 1873 but died the same year.[6] McKinley's wife descended into a deep depression at her baby's death and her health, never robust, declined.[6] Two years later, Katherine died of typhoid fever. Ida never recovered from their daughters' deaths, and the McKinleys had no more children.[6] Ida McKinley developed epilepsy around the same time and depended strongly on her husband's presence.[6] He remained a devoted husband and tended to his wife's medical and emotional needs for the rest of his life.[6]
