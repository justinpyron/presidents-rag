Clay and other war hawks demanded that the British revoke the Orders in Council, a series of decrees that had resulted in a de facto commercial war with the United States.[13] Though Clay recognized the dangers inherent in fighting Britain, one of the most powerful countries in the world, he saw it as the only realistic alternative to a humiliating submission to British attacks on American shipping.[14] Clay led a successful effort in the House to declare war against Britain, complying with a request from President Madison.[15] Madison signed the declaration of war on June 18, 1812, beginning the War of 1812.
